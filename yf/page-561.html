#14014 Midnight Rambler Sat, 11th Aug '12 10:42:24 AM from Delft
Out of curiosity, what are the rules for hebben and zijn?

Well, for a start, all transitive verbs (i.e. verbs that can have a direct object) take hebben, because for those verbs zijn would imply that it's passive:

'De wesp heeft gestoken' - The wasp has stung.
'De wesp is gestoken' - The wasp has been stung. *
presumably by a smaller wasp

'Ik heb overwonnen' - I have vanquished.
'Ik ben overwonnen' - I have been vanquished.

For intransitive verbs it's just random, as far as I know. That's the Dutch language for ya.

I remember from high school that in German, all verbs that imply movement (fahren, laufen, gehen, etc.) always take sein. In Dutch, this rule is applied far less rigorously. Hell, some verbs can even take EITHER hebben or zijn depending on the sentence.
