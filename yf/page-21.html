Aesthetics, character design, Animesque
Yeah, My Little Pony has always been kind of animesque. They have big eyes and Puni Plush now, but they had it back then, too. In fact, Generation 1 was made by Toei animation. It's at least as much of an anime as Little Nemo and various other things are.

And kind of up Lauren Faust's lane how Powerpuff Girls was blatantly inspired by anime, and even had an anime made of it. It looks almost as much of an anime as Panty and Stocking with Garterbelt does.

... which explains how, as a person who usually likes anime and tends to prefer the Japanese animation aesthetic, is gushing over this show's art style. It has a bit of a simplistic look like a lot of modern animation, has thick lines, and the like. But it's still really incredibly adorable. Kind of explains why I like the art style in Lilo and Stitch, as well.

Anyway, it may be simplistic and like a lot of modern American animation. It's definitely very well done. A lot of areas are well drawn and detailed for something with such supermassive thick lines and minimalism in many areas. Much like Foster's Home For Imaginary Friends. It may be simplistic, but there's a lot of detail put into things.
