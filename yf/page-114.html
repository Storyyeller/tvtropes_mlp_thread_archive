To be honest, I find the defensiveness displayed in some comments of Equestria Daily a little excessive. I mean, let's be honest, grown people watching a (well-made, mind you) little girls' show and writing fanfiction/drawing fanart about it is more than a little silly.

That's what makes it fun, of course, and there is nothing wrong with a bit of silliness anyway; but come on, it's not a tragedy if someone makes jokes about grownups watching My Little Pony: Friendship is Magic. That's precisely what we are doing ourselves, after all — I mean, the term "brony" itself is a clear enough hint, right? To say nothing about terms such as "everypony", "nopony" and so on...

In that first Fox segment, in particular, I think that the truly offensive thing was not that they compared bronies to spoiled manchildren — rather, it was that they used a bizarre (if amusing) Internet fandom as a way to insult people who go into disability because of very real, very serious conditions.

Add me to the legion of employed bronies, if you will. And since my day job consists in messing with some fairly interesting mathematical problems, I'd think that my personal contribution to humankind — small though it may be — is still greater than that of a bunch of unimaginative talk show denizens cracking predictable "jokes" about internet fandoms as an hamfisted attempt to advance their objectionable political beliefs.
