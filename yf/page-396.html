#9880 Carciofus Thu, 23rd Feb '12 2:33:46 PM from Canterlot
I never saw that being used for contradiction — sometimes for negation, as in C, although ¬ or overlining are usually preferred. The contradiction I am talking about is when you derive something to be both true and false, which is generally a sign that there is something wrong with your premises. So for example you can use it in proofs by contradiction — you know the idea, "Suppose that A is false .... blahblahblah .. then B is true and B is false. Therefore ⊥. Therefore A must be true."

Plus, ! has another meaning apart from the factorial. In resource-sensitive logics — basically, logics that keep track of how many times you use a certain premise — !P is the way for saying "use the premise P as many times as you need". For some weird reason, that meaning of ! is usually called "exponential", but eh...
