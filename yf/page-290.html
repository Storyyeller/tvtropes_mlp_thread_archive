I have no actual data to base this on, but here's how I think the people in charge see this situation:

Little kids don't want big box sets. Little kids see Ponies (or Barbie, or Bakugan, or what have you) on the DVD shelf of a toy store, they want ponies (etc). They don't care about whether it's one episode or twenty-six, because they're just dumb kids. However, parents aren't going to pay 40$/€ for some momentary impulse their kids get. And besides, the kids will probably get bored of it around the fifth episode.

Best sell the show to them in small doses. The parents are more likely to buy them, because the individual boxes are cheaper.
