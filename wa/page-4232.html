WARNING UNFOUNDED FANNON ABOUT TO APPEAR

My prefered system of goverment for Equestria would go like this:

There are three political bodies The House Of Nobles, who reign over regional areas in the Princess's name and keep personal retainer forces they loan to the crown when Equestria is in need of an Army, The House of Senators which is made up of elected ponies from the various regions and meets in Canterlot to pass bills and laws, and finally The House Of The Princesses (formerly House Of The Princess) which is made up of Celestia and Luna advised by a small council of ponies appointed by them.

The House Of Nobles is passed down by heredity and are charged with keeping the Princess's Peace, Standing Judgement over trials that local goverment (like the Mayors) doesn't have the authority to preside over or is biased to one side. The cannot introduce bills or laws to the House Of Senators, and must carry out the will of House Of Senators at all times, except when The Princess claims emergency powers of war.

The House Of Senators is elected independently by general vote. Each region getting x amount of Senators in relation to their population. The House Of Senators can pass bills and laws and is the only body with the power to declare a State Of War and grant emergency powers to the Princess. Once a bill garners a 51% or higher vote to pass it goes before the Princess who has the power to Veto it or sign it into law. If she vetos the bill it goes back to the Senate who can put it back before the Princess by reaching a second vote of 60% (and she can again veto it, next time making having it to reach a 70% vote to go to her again and so on) or they can send it before her Small Council, who if they reach a conses can overturn Celestia's veto.

Finally The House Of The Princesses is made of Celestia and a group of councilors (that never exceeds 13 in number) that the Princess appoints to their positions. This Council is entirely the Princess's choice, and usually has one pony from each Equestria's major industries or institutions. Once appointed a Councilor can only be dismissed by a 90% vote from the other Councilors that The Princess or her acting regent abstains from. The Princess is the only one who can call for a dismissal.
