#353139 Perpetual Lurker Tue, 2nd Apr '13 10:57:31 AM
@Sereg Nothing that hasn't already been implied by the flashback in A Canterlot Wedding. It does indirectly explore a bit of the ramifications of her becoming a princess at such a young age and what that means for her character, though.

As a princess, Candace was almost constantly approached with problems and issues that ponies wanted her help with, and she spent much of her time using her skills and magic to solve conflicts and help other ponies find love. Being so young, though, she was constantly told how great of a princess she would be, rather than what she was, and she never felt like a true leader. Eventually, she realized that she was constantly being led around herself, being given problems to solve or ponies to find love for and always deferring to the suggestions and commands of others. She had never sought out things that she wanted to fix, and never sought out her own love. She realized that in order to be a leader, she had to listen to her own heart, not just the hearts of others.
