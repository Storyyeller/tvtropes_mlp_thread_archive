To be honest, I would be perfectly comfortable with the series ending now. Serialized works, when they are popular and successful, are constantly in danger of Seasonal Rot, and what starts as "exploring side characters" or "world building" can become "padding" if one isn't very careful. And, invariably, the final result is a Door Stopper for anyone who does not experience the work as it is released: it's hard for the resulting product to feel tight or well-paced. Then there are the day-to-day commercial and executive constrains.

There are plenty of examples of serialized works that have attained greatness in compiled form. My hope is that Friendship Is Magic becomes one of those.
