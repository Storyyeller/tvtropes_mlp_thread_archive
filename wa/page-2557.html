I'm with Kegisak on thinking that the cutie mark simply represents a natural affinity for something that isn't necessarily a clear limit on their abilities. I think they get a few spells that they can perform intuitively, while other spells have to be learned. Take Rarity's gem-finding spell for example: she can do that spell more or less automatically because she has a natural affinity for gems and other fabulous things. Twilight can learn that ability, but she can't just do it by default. Think of it like learning a musical instrument. I'm pretty sure that almost anyone could learn to be competent at just about any instrument if they put the time into learning it, but most people tend to pick the one that they're best at or enjoy playing most and focus on it. Someone who's naturally gifted at playing guitar isn't automatically incapable of playing piano just because they don't have a gift for it, it just means they have to practice more.

I don't think it's possible for someone with Twilight's level of natural affinity (and, more importantly, her amount of practice) to study herself stupid, as I'm going to perhaps oversimplify Ginger's proposal. I agree with JTeeth that unicorns with magic-related cutie marks probably know their 'chosen' spell intuitively; they can learn other spells, too, but it takes more time and might be harder for them. Since Twilight's cutie mark is simply Magic, though, her mana reserve is much higher, she has less difficulty learning new spells, and she can probably learn more, period.

Of course, that doesn't mean she can upstage other unicorns in their special talents. Rarity's calling and talent isn't finding gems; it's design and dressmaking, which Twilight is patently useless at. And even if Twilight could learn Trixie's storytelling spells (assuming they're mostly spells and not just smoke-and-mirrors-style stagecraft), she doesn't have the knack for presentation that Trixie does. For all her strengths, she has plenty of shortcomings to make up for it.
