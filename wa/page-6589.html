@Ginger: Okay, well that's fair enough then. since you likely won't watch it anyways... Okay, so basically the whole 'horror movie' setup is being controlled by this foundation. They release chemicals that make the characters act in certain ways, have access to a host of monsters that the protagonists inadvertently choose from to come and kill them(The ones they end up choosing are pretty boring, just the zombies of some inbred redneck cultists). Basically the 'directors' are in control of everything. The reason for this, it happens, is because the whole thing is a ritual. The characters are sacrifices to the ancient ones, a host of primordial gods who willingly sealed themselves away on the condition that each year they receive sacrifices under specific rituals - each country has one set up, but several always fail so they've got plenty as backup. In America's case they have to bring in five youths who fit certain archetypes, have them go to the ritual grounds of their own volition and choose the method of their demise. they're then killed in a specific order to please the ancient ones. If the rituals aren't performed every year, the ancient ones basically rise up and torture the world to death. THIS year, every ritual has failed by the time America's starts(Except japan's, which fails halfway through).

However, because one of the characters is a stoner, he's immune to the effects of some of the gasses. Because of this, the ritual fails, and the ancient ones are released. There's a scene very close to the end where the ritual almost succeeds, BUT it would require one of the characters to murder the stoner in cold blood. So basically it's a matter of a severe Kick the Dog, or the end of the world. There's no real good way to end it, so the ending you get just feels like it works.
