#221669 Bluespade Tue, 12th Jun '12 12:16:44 AM from Texas
Well personally, I used to really be negative about Western Animation in general. I was a huge anime fan in my teenage days. Pretty much the only western cartoon I ever got into was Invader Zim, and even that one I only liked about half of the episodes. So I was pretty much an anime snob, I think.

That changed when I saw Avatar: The Last Airbender, one of the best shows of any sort I've ever seen. It kind of opened my mind to the possibilities of western animation, and since it actually was successful I kept hoping that it would spark some kind of revolution in western animation, so that more shows with strong overarching stories would get produced. That...didn't happen, really, but in case it did, I started keeping an eye on the whole western animation scene, hoping for a good show to come out. So I started hearing lots of good things about this show.

I wanted to like it. I dunno if I'm weird, but it never really bothered me that I might enjoy a show made for girls, so I eagerly checked it out. Unfortunately, I didn't really care for it.

So I stayed away for about 4 months, but since I'm online a lot, I came across more and more pony related stuff all over the place. I got to know all the characters and their personalities without even watching the show, and I started to get intrigued. I decided to give the show another try, and though my initial watch through was somewhat rocky after about 10 episodes I was pretty much hooked.

Still, it wouldn't be anything more than just another show I've watched if I hadn't discovered this thread, which greeted me enthusiastically and has become my favorite place to hang out online.

#221672 Bluespade Tue, 12th Jun '12 12:23:37 AM from Texas
Things I like about it:

1) Very good characters. Strong personalities, strong flaws, (mostly) consistent characterization. It's rare to come across this anywhere let alone in children's television.

2) The happy, bright, optimistic tone. The vast majority of things I watch/read/play tend to be rather dark. I like dark fiction a lot, but I get really tired of it if it's the only thing I take in. I really like to have some cute, happy stuff to balance it out. Plus I've always liked cute things.

3) The unique, well thought out fantasy setting. I love fantasy, but in general most settings tend to be rather samey, falling into the same old cliches or just retreading once-brilliant ideas until they lose whatever uniqueness they once had. One thing that can't be denied about MLP is it's not like any other fantasy series out there. There aren't even any humans in it!

Oh yeah, and it's genuinely funny.
