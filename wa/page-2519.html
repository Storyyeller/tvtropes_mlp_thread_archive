#62958 Badwolfwho Sat, 31st Dec '11 5:24:20 PM
Yeah like Civilisation. But IN SPACE!. It's really good.

Basically I'll always pick the moral option available despite the costs it has. I'll never declare war and have only a minor military in comparison to my civ's size. All of the perks my species have relates to loyalty, culture, happiness and ecology. None for war. My population's happiness must always be in the green and thus taxes need to be low.

One problem is that having a low military means the other civs think I'm easy to push around but I'm sure that Princess Celestia (my head of state) wouldn't just hand over a planet of her ponies to unrepentant slavers. Thus they declare war, and then so do all of their allies (they have a few). Luckily they live quite far away from me and I own most of the habitable planets in my core region so it will be hard for them to get to me.

The other problem is with being a race with a small military. I can't win via conquest, there are other ways to win of course, technological, cultural and via ascension. I got lucky and started next to an Ascension Crystal so I just have to hold out, protect the starbase I have there and in late game I will ascend and win. This has the problem that every race in the game is opposed to me since I am already trying to win.

But since I've specialised into culture rather than warfare my planets will rarely flip to my neighbours side and theirs often do, especially when they build in my neck of the woods.
