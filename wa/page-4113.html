#102814 kegisak Mon, 20th Feb '12 5:37:14 PM
@Fallen: I'm a game designer by trade. Games, as an industry, are... weird. You tend to have very clear era's of dominant games, because big budget games cost millions of dollars to make. In order to make that back, you need to be popular, so you have to do what's popular at the time. Which is why it seems like all the big budget games today are shooters or hack-n-slash. The next era comes along as sales start to decline and someone tries something that's new and successful. Basically, the industry needs to pander to what's popular and basically copy each other in order to turn a profit. Smart consumers see through it, but the stupid ones are too numerous to turn away.

Of course soem companies find other ways of doing it. EA basically subsisted on sports games for years, and you know how? By establishing 4 or 5 major franchises and basically re-releasing them once a year. Madden, NHL, SOFA, they're basically all the same games year after year with a graphics upgrade. Cheap to make, and your fanbase keep coming back, year after year.

It's cheap, and it runs on the fact that consumers are stupid enough to keep coming back.
