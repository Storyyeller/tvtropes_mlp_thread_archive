#46191 Kayeka Mon, 28th Nov '11 3:05:21 PM from Amsterdam from Amsterdam
Well, since someone already brought tabletop roleplaying games into our ponies, I might as well speculate on what the mane 6's class and alignment would be if they were in a Dungeons & Dragons 3.5 game (because 4e is too low on fluff to be any fun in that regard).

  • Twilight Sparkle: Lawful Neutral Wizard. Lawful since she is such a stickler for rules. The 'neutral' will probably see some discussion if anyone actually cares about this, but the way I see it, not only have I never seen Twilight go out of her way to help someone other then her friends, she was also far too willing to simply make a problem once once she felt she'd benefit from it. Her ridiculously powerful innate talent for magic would suggest sorcerer, but she goes about learning magic the wizard way: With many, many books.
  • Applejack: Lawful Good Fighter. The way she values truth and honesty qualifies her as lawful, and she actually does go out of her way to help anyone in need. Being incredibly strong, she'd probably be a physical unit. While I was tempted to peg her as a Paladin because of her probably better then average wisdom score*
    and also because paladin is my favourite class, and Applejack is best pony
    , I just can't see her caring much for 'divine justice' because of her down-to-earth attitude. Barbarian doesn't fit either, so we'll go with fighter.
  • Rarity: Neutral Good Sorcerer. She only seems to care about rules or etiquette when someone is looking, but she is incredibly generous to anyone once she's figured out what she is supposed to give. The sorcerer's charisma-based spellcasting*
    and wisdom as a dumpstat
    seems like a perfect fit for her.
  • Fluttershy: Neutral Good Druid. Not enough of a spine to be either lawful or chaotic, but she seems particularly fond of spreading the good wherever she goes. Deep connection to nature, but would probably be more comfortable supporting her party by casting spells as a druid, rather then directly dealing damage like a ranger would. Well, that one was easy.
  • Rainbow Dash: Chaotic Neutral Ranger. Apart from her arrogance-induced stint as Ponyville's hero, she doesn't really seems to care much about doing the right thing, and even skipped out on helping one of her friends because she was busy napping, so going for full good is pretty much out of the question. Plays by her own rules, and unnecessarily charges straight into danger because she felt like it, so chaotic. Her class was a bit more difficult, but in the end, I settled for something that would benefit from a huge dexterity score, did not involve sneaking around, and would allow her to charge ahead with not one, but TWO weapons.
  • Pinkie Pie: Chaotic Good Bard. She wants to see smiles on absolutely everyone's faces, and won't take no for an answer. I'm also pretty sure no one will argue on her being chaotic. And someone whose main addition to the team appears to be a raise in morale and starts singing in weird moments? Pinkie Pie was born a bard.

I think I just out-geeked 90% of the internet population.
