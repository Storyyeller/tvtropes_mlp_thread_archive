#317868 kegisak Sun, 16th Dec '12 4:06:57 PM
Fun fact! Bats actually have perfectly fine vision, comparable to humans. They just hunt at night and never had to develop night vision.

Most animals with proper night vision have something called a Tapetum Lucidum, which causes the glowing eye effect. While this improves night-time vision, due to increased light, it actually hampens daytime vision due to how it works - it's a reflective film, meaning it's semi-opaque. So during the day an animals vision is basically like looking through a foggy window.
