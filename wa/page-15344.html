#383579 kegisak Tue, 16th Jul '13 2:18:36 PM
Death is one of those things, in my opinion, where you have to pay really close attention to what you're saying when you use it. Especially if you're going to have it deliberate on someone's part, because if that someone is a hero it kind of implies the dead deserved it, which by proxy can be taken to preach that X class of people deserve to die. Now, if you do go only for the truly evil you're not exactly gonna offend many people, but even so.

Consider the case of One Piece: Eichiro Oda deliberately chooses not to kill any of his villains because it doesn't really prove anything, except that Luffy happens to be stronger. In order to make the good guys actually win, he demolishes the bad guy's morals and proves him wrong.
