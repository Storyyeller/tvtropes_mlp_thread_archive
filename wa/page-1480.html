I think that the "hat" that unicorns wear is magic - they seem to be the only ones to do it on a daily basis, even if in extraordinary circumstances the other ponies can do a little bit of "artifact" style magic like they do in the first episode. Also, since advanced magic seems to require a horn, only unicorns can really do it a lot (their horns are like wands, from what I can tell), while the rest of the ponies have what could be described more as supernatural talents instead of Hermetic Magic, like Fluttershy's skills with beasts and Rainbow Dash's weather manipulation. This also puts the unicorns and pegasus-unicorns in a position of power over the other ponies, if only because they can use telekinesis to manipulate small objects, like sewing needles - the other ponies don't seem to use tools all that much, though it is implied that somehow Fluttershy can sew with just her hooves and mouth (maybe she hypnotizes her pets into doing it for her??).

Also, griffons are jerks, which actually makes a lot of sense if you look at classic mythology. In the original myths, griffons were the natural enemies of horses. In fact, the hippogryph, a combination of horse and griffon, came about when a greek poet wanted a metaphor for mocking an alliance between Athens and Sparta, so he decided to go with "Athens and Sparta working together is like a griffon sleeping with a horse".

^^Earth ponies have been described as having a sort of innate natural magic that's difficult to pin down; people have alternately explained it as everything from super growing skills to a really strong connection with the earth.

Pegasi have what appears to be a sort of tactile telekinesis; they can fly and they can carry really heavy things in the air, which means that they probably make things lighter when they touch them. Their natural magic is also why they can touch clouds and stuff like that.

The alicorn sisters are special cases; they embody all three races, so they presumably hold the magic of all three as well.

And yes, all the races are socially equal. Notice how there aren't any unicorns on the planting team in "Winter Wrap-Up" (at least I don't think there are)? Each of the races does what they're most suited to do, and unicorns, while capable of fine telekinesis, aren't as sturdy as earth ponies and aren't as strong.
