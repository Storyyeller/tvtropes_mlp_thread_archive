#108085 Ace Of Scarabs Sun, 26th Feb '12 9:25:06 AM from Singapore
Sonic used to barely break Mach 1 because his running speed was restricted by the physics engine, then he trained hard and broke Mach 5 by Sonic 3. Now, in the post-Unleashed era, with the advent of the Boost power, he can easily cruise at said supersonic speeds without the need for altitude diving.

Dashie is fast and all, but her specialty is her rapid acceleration and aerobatics-friendly agility, which lets her dart about rapidly and take turns tightly before accelerating out. Her Rainboom speed is achieved with serious dive acceleration, so it's more like Samus's Speed Booster/Shine Spark technique in that she needs a good prep and run-up to reach that speed.

Also, Sonic can move fast enough he breaks physics and telefrags himself on "Robotnik's Deadly Speed Traps" by sheer virtue of running faster than even Blast Processing could handle, or somehow accidentally clipping out of bounds and falling into the Void between worlds. The most Dashie has done to herself is crash badly enough to lay herself up for a few days with a broken wing.

Granted, these days Sonic manipulates his ability to harness the power of Chaos Emeralds to the point that by Generations, he no longer even needs to go Super to give Perfect Chaos a humiliating headache, and he even easily trashed the completed Egg Dragoon without brutal Werehog strength. He's older and of a higher experience level, so it's unfair to compare Modern Sonic to Dashie thataway.

In a serious race on a course that favours both of them equally with heights and steep dive points and numerous twists-and-turns and lots of parkour-friendly obstacles, it would be fun to watch both of them show off against each other. Mostly I think it's unfair to compare them though, since as I mentioned Sonic's waaaay more experienced than Dashie is. I figure they'll end up friendly rivals :3

Also we aren't fractured like the Sonic Fandom, which has Purists, Cartoon Hounds, Furries, and Crazy Fanon Clowns and Haters shooting at each other with flamethrowers.

#108086 Light Phaser Sun, 26th Feb '12 9:27:27 AM from The Fantasy Zone
Okay, fine, you know what? Why not

The reason so many people enjoy the franchise (in this particular case, the original trilogy) is quite simple: Good ol' platforming. Tons of good ol' platforming. You may say "What the hell, Light Phaser? Sonic's about speed!", and although that's true, look at the very first game! It was rather slow, but still an enjoyable experience for the most part. Sonic 2 cranked things up. Hell, it was one of the first games to use Sega's famous "Blast Processing" marketing gimmick, and it shows. Finally, Sonic 3 and Knuckles were mere months apart, so I'll talk about them together. This was finally when Sega "fine tuned" the series, as this was when the franchise got big. Literally! The stages were larger, alternate paths were now everywhere, and KNUCKLES, guys! KNUCKLES!

So there. Why I like Sonic.
