#123171 Japanese Teeth Mon, 12th Mar '12 10:04:21 PM from Meinong's jungle
I think it's a probably a combination of both. The show definitely hasn't gotten worse, but I think the elements that are emphasized are more likely to draw strong reactions. The first season episodes were more subdued and safe; there aren't as many people with strong dislike, but a lot of them don't have the flash that triggers that sort of BEST EPISODE EVER reaction. I think the writers and animators are taking way more risks this season. It mostly pays off, but when it doesn't, it's more noticeable. It basically comes down to personal preference more than anything else anyway. There are more Love It or Hate It elements this time around. Lesson Zero is a good example; most people either consider it one of their favorites (like myself), and some find it a low point. There are people who are sort of meh on it, but they're in the minority. And the divide is kinda understandable; I like it because the premise clicked with me and I thought that the over-the-top ness was an interesting deviation from how the show normally works. Obviously, some people just don't care for that style of humor.
