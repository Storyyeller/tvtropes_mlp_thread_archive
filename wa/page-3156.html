As kinda cute as Spike's crush on rarity is, I really want to see Spike grow past it. Not because I have any issues with a dragon hooking up with a pony, but because he needs to realize his crush is in part childish infatuation.

Ginger, I'd argue crying or sorrow can be cathartic. Take the film adaptation of The Fox and the Hound for example, that's a beautiful statement on friendship, yet people inevitably cry at it's conclusion, not because it's deeply depressing, but because it's cathartic. Another example is The Yiddish Policeman's Union, the lead characters essentially fail in most of their goals, yet the ending still carries a good deal of catharsis for the reader. Another good example is Philip Dick's The Simulacra, humanity is on the verge of destruction, yet a few people have uncovered the objective truth of the world and learned the existential human need people have of confiding and connecting with people. The sadness does not overshadow the ending, instead it encourages catharsis and the admission of loneliness so that we may be able to interact with others on a more meaningful level and so that we find the comfort in each other we may otherwise ignore.
