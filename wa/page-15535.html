#388353 kegisak Tue, 6th Aug '13 8:08:04 AM
Well...

That is...

Well, look. Execution trumps concept by a mile. This is something that was hammered into my in college - I studied video games, and you bet your ass that everyone under the sun has 'a great idea for a video game', and 90% don't pan out because they have a great idea for a story. but not a game. So regardless of the nature of your story, if you pull it off well, it'll be a good story.

Concept-wise, though? It sounds like a million other stories before it. The Human in Equestria genre, especially in a wish-fulfilment context, has been done so often that most people won't touch it, and a lot will downvote it on principle. Now, him going into Discord's care instead of, say, Twilight's, is vaguely novel, and it's be interesting to see where it goes from there, but it's not going to be enough to get people to give it a chance on it's own.

Now, writing for upvotes isn't really a good idea, and I it's a story you want to write, then cool, go for it. I recommend writing whatever you damn well feel like, really. Just don't expect to be the next big thing, I guess?
