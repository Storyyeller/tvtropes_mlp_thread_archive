#159860 Enlong Tue, 17th Apr '12 9:36:36 AM from The Magitek Holyland
Goodness you people post fast.

Why would I be worried about the tower?

Death represents great change, while The Tower represents ruin. Also, chaos, crisis, impact, etc.

It's not a fun card. See, the imagery is of a tower being struck by lightning and splitting apart. The tower represents things that have been built up (perhaps on false foundations) being torn down.

Fun fact: in Harry Potter and the Half Blood Prince, the astute among you may remember that Professor Trelawny found herself repeatedly pulling The Tower on the same night that the thing that everyone remembers from that book happened. In fact, the chapter where it actually happens is titled "The Lightning-Struck Tower". Fun stuff.

The last few cards in the deck follow an interesting progression. We have The Devil, which represents temptation, vice, and obsession, followed by the Tower, representing crisis and ruin. A Darkest Hour kind of thing. Then, we get The Star, The Moon, and The Sun. Three cards of increasing strength, each symbolising inner qualities, and, in two of them, hope and joy. Then, we have Judgement, which represents rebirth and salvation, and finally, The World, perhaps the most positive card in the deck.
