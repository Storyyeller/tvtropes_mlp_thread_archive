Mmm... I can definitely see someone liking the art, but I dunno. It's not for me. Those legs man, they just keep jumping off the page at me. Unless it's because it's doing something really cool and weird, the art in a comic should compliment the story, not distract from it.

Sandman's first two volumes are probably the best example I can point to. Gaiman and the artists do amazing things with the panel layout and the art that really lends itself to the dreamland setting, and on their own their gorgeous and terrifying and surreal, but in the context of reading it you barely notice them because the changes are always triggered by or related to the story, so it feels natural rather than some real 'event'. Those backwards legs just stick out at me and stop me thinking about the story.
