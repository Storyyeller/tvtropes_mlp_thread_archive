#86358 Enlong Thu, 2nd Feb '12 4:31:05 PM from The Magitek Holyland
I like Rarity because it's very clear that she really wants to be a good person.

Nobody can deny that she slips. There are episodes, like Sonic Rainboom, where she gets caught up in something else and loses track of her initial goal. Heck, in that episode, she was the one most determined to help Rainbow, to the point of becoming a magic guignea pig, before she got caught up in the dangerous fabulousness of the wings.

The thing of it is, though, is that she understands when she's doing something wrong. For example, in Green Isn't Your Color, she realizes immediately that she's being unfairly jealous of Fluttershy's success, and while she can't stop herself from feeling that way, she understands that she isn't in the right, and does what she can to keep quiet and let Fluttershy enjoy her fame. In some other show, a character in that situation probably wouldn't be so self-aware until the end of the episode.

Heck, in Suited For Success, she makes the dresses exactly as her friends want, despite it offending every sensibility she has as a designer, because it's what her friends want, and she is going to give it to them if it drives her insane.

Yes, she's a bit vain, yes she's a bit greedy, and yes, she has a bit of a problem with losing focus. But she wants to be a good person, and when she backs up and looks at the big picture, she's perfectly willing to admit when she's wrong and do the right thing, no matter what it takes.

And I think she's good enough at what she does to justify a little preening, wouldn't you agree?
