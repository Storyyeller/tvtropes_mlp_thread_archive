“The moon. We’re on the moon. The moon is space, yeees?” said the one with the yellow eye, in a voice that was, to be honest, kind of creepy

“Yes, the moon is in space, ” Said the one with the blue eye.

“Moon. Moon-space. Space-moon.”

“What, ” Nightmare Moon said, walking up to them, “is this?” The two orbs turned and stared at her.

“Umm…” said the blue one, obviously trying to think.

“Hi, space unicorn…” said the yellow one.

“Shut up, let me do the talking, ” hissed the blue one, before turning back to Nightmare Moon, “Uh, hi. We are, um, real estate agents, and we’re just here to give you an evaluation on this fine piece of property.”

“Ponies. Space ponies…” muttered the yellow one.

“So, we’d just like to say you have a lovely hoOOOOO-” The blue one was cut off when it and its yellow companion were lifted off the ground, wrapped in the dark blue aura.

“You’re lying, ” said Nightmare Moon, holding the blue one up to her face. For a mechanical contraption, it was obviously frightened, “what are you doing here?”

“I’m in spaaaaaaaace!” said the yellow one ecstatically. Nightmare Moon just had to stare at it.

“He likes space, ” said the blue one.

“Space space space space space… space horse, space unicorn…”

“I will ask one one last time: what are you doing here?” Nightmare Moon asked, but she had no time to make an accompanying threatening expression, because the blue one cracked.

“We were sent here because we did something wrong and I’m sorry and I wish she’d forgive us and please don’t hurt me!”

“Not sorry. I’m in space. Love space, love space…”

Nightmare Moon paused, thinking about it. So, these two had been banished here, just like her.

“But…” the blue one said, resuming an earlier thread of conversation, “we must say that you have a lovely home.”
