#259268 Irish Zombie Thu, 26th Jul '12 7:42:03 PM from Texas
@storyyeller Huh. That's interesting. In 5, there are ten social branches, which you unlock through spending accumulated culture, and which are seperated by ages. In the stone age, there is Tradition (good for small empires), Liberty (good for expansion), and Honor (good for armies). Then you get Piety (good for culture) in the Classical age, Patronage (good for city-state diplomacy) and Commerce (good for seafaring and gold) in the Medieval age, and Rationalism (science, incompatible with Piety) soon after. Then, you have your choice between Freedom, Autocracy and Order, which can't be used together.

So, ironically, a monarchy (which might be rolled into Tradition or Piety) probably isn't a good choice for a warmonger.

In that case, don't let him hear about the Equestrian civ mod.
