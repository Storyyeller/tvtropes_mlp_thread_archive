#66849 Mio Sat, 7th Jan '12 8:41:13 PM
A lot of people talk about how flaws make a character great, but I think that to certain extent they miss the point. Yes a flaws make a character seem more real and relateble, on the other hand they make for very good reasons for why we shouldn't like them. I always look at a character's flaws as their potential to grow and become stronger. If that potential isn't tapped in some way and the character remains static then he seizes to be interesting and becomes grating (admittedly this is not always the case).

That is kind of the problem with RD. We see her learn lessons, but to be honest do you think she really changed at all? MMDW was most egregious about this, which is sad because i think it did more to develop her then people think. Like J Teeth said they have been really focusing on her negative traits, mostly because they make for great comedic devices, but at the expense of her supposed good traits and the impression that she is capable of change at all.
