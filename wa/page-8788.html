@Lurker: What, the teachers not caring or the US's issues? Because the US's issues are more cultural than anything, not educational.

But I'm sure that a fair amount of the problem does lie in the system. Yes, there are bad apples in every system - my elementary school principle was one of them, doing such things as trying to control my brother's ritalin medication and overmedicate him.

But a bad system will cause a lot of problems where they didn't exist before. The fact that teachers in the US barely make enough to survive puts a lot of strain on them, and the fact that a lot of school have generally very poor funding means that the strain is compounded by the fact that they simply don't have the resources to be doing a lot of things they should to keep things running smoothly. The teachers don't really make enough to care because their worried for their own future, and the schools are encouraged to keep things running 'smoothly' and not to kick up the system too much. I recall reading a news story about a place in the US where a rule forbade teachers from speaking about homosexuality in any context that would imply them taking a side on it, meaning they weren't able to counsel gay students being bullied or to reprimand their bullies - leading to a spike in suicides.

Now that's not happening everywhere, obviously, but it's an example. The system is making it very difficult for the teachers to be doing all the things they could and should be doing.
