Fahrenheit converts to Celcius at a different scale, depending on where it is. So 30C is like 90F, but -30C is -30F. IT makes it baffling to me people actually us Fahrenheit, because it must be incredibly hard to measure if the difference between degrees is different every time.
The thing that causes that is the fact that zero degrees isn't the same temperature on the Celsius scale as it is on the Fahrenheit scale. Zero degrees Celsius is based on the freezing point of water, while zero degrees Fahrenheit is based on the freezing point of a specific mixture of brine (if my research is accurate).
