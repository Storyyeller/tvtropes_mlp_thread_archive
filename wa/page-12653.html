#316313 Pykrete Thu, 13th Dec '12 4:05:43 PM from Viridian Forest
Playing through this incredibly generically titled RPG. It's...pretty dull and poorly balanced, and I'm not sure how much of is nostalgic suck as a parody of Final Fantasy 1 and how much of it is just poorly planned suck.

The world map is huge but obscenely sparse, so while the game is several hours long, most of it is just walking around the overworld looking for one of the 3-4 towns in the game. There are a lot of places that look like there was going to be a cave or something tucked away there, but never got put in.

Items are hideously overpriced for as little good as they do. Apples that restore less than half of your HP cost more than armor and weapons, and status cures cost several times as much.

Status effect magic is useless and almost never even lands properly on common enemies. Offensive magic is a complete joke. Twilight's spells do less damage than Fluttershy's regular attack, Fluttershy's summons do less damage than her own regular attack. Both do several times less than Pinkie, AJ, and Dash. So basically, Twilight is a buff bitch for them. Most of the buffs are useless, having either negligible effect or no particularly useful one ‚Äî for instance, Dash gets a self-target speed buff but has no need for it because she's already faster than pretty much everything in the game. So basically, everyone just spams their regular attack until one side or the other is dead while Twilight tries to look useful and Rarity desperately tries to keep up heals that don't scale properly with enemy damage, and the fight depends entirely on incoming damage spreading itself in the most fortunate possible way among six targets.

The main thing I got out of this game is just more bitterness that MLP Online got C&D'd, because as of yet it's the only pony RPG I've seen that wasn't so half-assed.

#316320 Perpetual Lurker Thu, 13th Dec '12 4:33:18 PM
It's not so much the characters that are the problem. It's the trademark. Trademarks are an "enforce or you lose it" type deal, whereas copyrights can be enforced at one's leisure. It's why no one ever goes after fan artists or writers unless they make a business out of selling their work. Once you start producing something big with "My Little Pony" in the title, though, then they're more or less forced to step in to protect the trademark on the name. Fighting is Magic and other fan games should be safe, insofar that Hasbro doesn't have any motive to shut them down.

It's the same reason that the Shards of Equestria Magic: The Gathering fan set was shut down. The MTG symbols are all trademarked.
