Here's the thing. I don't have a scanner at the moment, so I can't get good clean pics. This means the filler tool won't work properly, and so I have to resort to pure brush work. Hence the scratchy, sketchy look.
Two alternative ways to accomplish that:
  1. If you're using the tablet to trace over the lines for your outlines, do that on a separate layer from your photographed or scanned sketch. On that layer, select the interior of your outlines, then fill in that selection on yet another separate layer. If your outlines are aliased at all, then "grow" your selection a few pixels before filling in, and make sure the layer for the interior is below the layer for the borders.
  2. There ought to be a way in Photoshop to reduce the color depth to 1-bit. So everything is pure black or pure white. (I know how to do it in GIMP but I've never used Photoshop.) Import your picture much, much bigger than you want the finished image to be; reduce the color depth to 1-bit; clean up any stray pixels; then bump the color depth back up to maximum so you can color it in. Then shrink it down to the desired size (which will eliminate the jagged pixels of your borders that resulted from the conversion to 1-bit.)
