#41339 CDRW Fri, 18th Nov '11 3:30:29 PM
@ Ginger. Present tense has a lot of attributes that make it really difficult to tell a normal story. The main issue is that in present tense, every action in the story is happening now. The opening of the story is now, the climax of the story is now, and the ending is now. In present tense it's difficult to show the passing of time, it's difficult to establish a flow to the story, and it can get really tiresome to have everything happening now.

Also there's the fact that the written word is ultimately derived from the spoken word. You don't tell a campfire story in the present tense because in the present you are around the fire listening to a storyteller. You tell a story as either "I did, " or "he did." It makes it harder to establish suspension of disbelief if you're saying "I'm doing, " or "he's doing, " and that difficulty translates over to written works as well.
