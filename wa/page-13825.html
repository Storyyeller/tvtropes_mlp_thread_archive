It's still possible to do a decent-scale pony game provided they keep it quiet throughout the entirety of development. The problem is a solid game will typically take a couple years of development. That means a) we lost all the ones that have been in progress and had groundwork laid since the fandom showed up, b) any new game starting up now will somehow have to fly completely under the radar for another couple years, which is pretty much impossible to do while accumulating staff and testers, especially once the project is in late stages and you naturally lose and gain some (I assure you Hasbro's lawyers have an eye on Equestria Gaming, I mean really now), c) most of our developers have a raging hardon for multiplayer games that will need persistent servers that will immediately get C&D'd after the game comes out, and d) we won't see anything that starts now done for a couple years, likely beyond the end of the series and the fandom starts to disperse.

Even under the best of circumstances, unless the main show has an epically monster run like Spongebob or something, yeah our large-scale fangames are pretty much done. The Western IP gestapo hasn't even lost momentum.
