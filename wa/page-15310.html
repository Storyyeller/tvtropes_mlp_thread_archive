Not quite. 'Ulster' (of which Northern Ireland is six of it's nine counties) is predominately protestant and loyal to the crown (loyalist). The rest of Ireland is predominately Catholic. In the past, the Catholic majority had no right to vote, hold public office, carry firearms, go to school etc after the introduction of the Penal Laws (repelled in the 1830's) following defeat of the (English but Catholic) King James' army at the Battle Of The Boyne (River) in 1690. The 12th is about celebrating all that.

Many claim that the Orange Order are like the KKK if they celebrated the establishment of the Jim Crow laws. After partition in 1921 (were six counties of Ulster became Northern Ireland under British rule and the rest eventually became the republic of Ireland, a separate country) the Orange Order liked to remind those Catholics living in N.I who was in charge...with violence and intimation. It helped that like 90% of the Order's members where also members of the paramilitary Ulster Volunteer Force. Some think the IRA caused the troubles of 1969-1994/2007...what actually happened was that the UVF were shocked at Catholic Nationalists daring to ask for an end to discrimination (inspired by Doctor King's movement in the 60s) and celebrating 50 years of Irish independence...and promptly started murdering them left right and center. Cue IRA number three rising up to fight back.

@ keg

First point, yes.

Second point, there have been numerous IRA incarnations. The first was the one who fought the British in the south west of Ireland in 1919-20, and who forced them to propose partition. After that it split, because some said sweet, we're independent now, and others said nah screw that it's all of Ireland or nothing. They fought the Irish civil war of 1920-21, the anti-treaty forces were defeated and scattered, and the pro-treaty forces became the new Irish State (including the army). Then the second IRA showed up in the new state of N.I to end partition, and for many years did...absolutely nothing. Few failed bomb plots and taking pot shots at police. That's it. Then after Bloody Sunday in 1972, they were reborn as the Provisional IRA (the Provos) and they got teeth. The Provos are the famous one. Then after the 1994r cease fire they split again. The new group were called The Real IRA and carried on fighting...until they too jacked it in around 2007...only for some new groups to show up. But these new groups are not the IRA...the old Provo force was thousands strong, armed to the teeth with guns from America and Libya (everything from Armilites to A K47s to RP Gs) and for a few years in the early 70s kept the british army out of areas of the country. The new groups are about a dozen people...maybe 200 tops...with some pistols and a few bombs.
