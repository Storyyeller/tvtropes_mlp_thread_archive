#352237 kegisak Sat, 30th Mar '13 4:59:02 PM
In my experience the best practice is usually in as wide a series of poses as you can get. Ideally dynamic, to study how the body moves, acts, and reacts, but you don't want to go overboard with it. Draw characters in realistic, real-life scenarios, stuff they would be doing throughout the day. In fact, drawing someone through every step of their day could actually be a very good exercise.

The largest recorded jump in my artistic ability came when I attempted a graphic novel of sorts. It had a pretty wide variety of poses due to being composed pretty evenly of action scenes and life scenes, so I highly recommend that approach.

Also, believe it or not, saucy and smutty pics are excellent for learning anatomy. I've heard of people studying porn and drawing it just to get a feel for how the actors move.
