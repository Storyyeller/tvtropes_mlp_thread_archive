#115829 CDRW Mon, 5th Mar '12 10:07:48 AM
Big, sappy, personal, almost, but not quite, offtopic post. Spoilered for those who just want ponies. I know it's coming out of nowhere, but I feel like it's something I should share and you guys are good enough friends to appreciate what it means.

I actually had an important insight into myself last night. For several years now, I've been thinking about the kind of person I want to be, specifically, what kind of person I would be if I extrapolated my basic personality into the best possible version of myself. Over time I accumulated a large, somewhat nebulous collection of traits and an overall impression, but it was never concrete enough for my satisfaction.

Then last night, I started wondering why it was so easy for me to write that Fancy Pants letter in my blog, while I struggle to write a single line of dialogue for characters who I've analyzed more and understand much better than him. Everything from the language to the progression of the letter, to the balance of dignity and resolve to do the right thing came out on the first try. That's when it hit me. I hadn't really tried to write that from Fancy Pants's view, I'd tried to write it from the view of my "ideal self, " because he's so similar to a lot of the things I want to see in myself.

It wouldn't be strictly true to say I've recieved life direction from this show, because all the thoughts and feelings involved pre-date my entrance into the fandom, but it's served as the catalyst for insight. In that moment of realization, the big cloud of fuzzy ideas folded into itself and became a single, solid, usable insight into myself, and for the first time in my life I was able to look at something and say clearly, "This is who I want to be."
