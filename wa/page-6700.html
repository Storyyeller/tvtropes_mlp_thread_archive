While I'm posting random observations: A lot of people were dissapointed that we received no explanation for Cadance being an Alicorn, and no further elaboration on just what it means to be a Princess in Equestria. I actually feel the opposite. A lot of scifi/fantasy works rely heavily on exposition and info dumps to characterize their settings. This often leads to the focus being on the world, not the plot or characters, creating a sterile, dull story regardless of how fascinating the setting might be. MLP:FIM on the other hand, presents simple facts and allows the viewer to infer the rest of the information about the setting. I greatly prefer this style. It causes the viewer to become more personally involved with the setting, since they have to think about it a bit, and also does a good job of preventing accidental plot holes.

Fully agreed, I just wish they would use their sparing time better so the episodes can have a better ending. Endings are my little quirk, I put a LOT of stock in endings. A bad ending can change my opinion of an entire work.
