Yeah, for the most part it's there for first looks, so it's not liek they're ever gonna do anythign that huge. Nobody wants to risk their focus trailers getting overshadowed by something else at a huge event like that, I think.

As for the console issues... Well, probably the big one is a sort of perceived loss of freedom with your games - the system has really specific DRM, so a game can only be 'loaned' to one person, and it won't work for anyone else, then. There's been some question about whether or not used games will be viable, though I think the conclusion they've now come to is that there'll be a fee for used, or something.

Games have to be loaded onto Xbox live last time I heard, and can only be played from there, but Gold still requires a fee - though there'll still be the free accounts I don't doubt. Still, for many it's cause for concern because it basically means all your games are still in Microsoft's hands.

And then there's the camera and the connectivity... Xbox needs to check in at least once a day, meaning that you need to have at least a relatively stable internet connection, or you'll be unable to play - literally, the Xbox will refuse to play if you can't check in. While the console is on, the Kinect Camera, which comes bundled, is always on, and it must be connected for the Xbox to work. It can be 'paused', whatever that means, but not turned off without turning off the system.

In the initial press release it also looked like they were basically ignoring the game aspect of it in general, in favour of showing off how it could control your TV, or Skype and the like. On the whole I don't think it'll wind up being THAT convenient, but the system still concerns me based on the precedent it sets, because if it succeeds(Which is kinda unlikely) then Microsoft might feel like pushing further next time.
