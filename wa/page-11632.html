#290795 Pykrete Thu, 4th Oct '12 5:49:08 PM from Viridian Forest
Typesafe means it doesn't blindly let you use classes wrong. In oversimplified terms, it's totally possible to futz with data by its location instead of by its structure (essentially like trying to do brain surgery by ignoring the shape of the brain then closing your eyes and poking around while your assistant nurse shouts coordinates at you), but it's usually a really bad idea and this language stops you from doing it on accident.

By robust I just mean it can do some of the neat stuff you'd normally see in less ridiculous languages, like exception handling (you attempt to do an operation that might be invalid, but have a contingency block of code to run if it fails — the try/catch blocking they mention).

Yeah it'd be hell to write in just because it takes like 4x as much text to do anything, but it's still funny.
