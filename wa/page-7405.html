#185111 Bluespade Fri, 4th May '12 9:17:55 PM from Texas
There's a tenant in film making that a work should stand on its own

I think this is totally true... from a FILM MAKER'S point of view. The reason they want films to be able to stand alone is so that no prospective audience members will feel like they shouldn't go see it. It's just about maximizing the size of the audience, for the sake of making more profit. Which is fine, nothing wrong with making money.

From an AUDIENCE'S point of view though, looking at a film solely as a work of entertainment, any kind of experimental style is valid, if it's good. The Avengers style, with the intro to the characters split up into individual movies, is great from an audience viewpoint as long as each of the movies is entertaining enough to stand on it's own.

Anyway, tenets in general deserve to be reevaluated regularly, there's no better way for art to become stale and overdone than by blindly accepting traditions as gospel.

/Opinions.
