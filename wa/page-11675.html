#291858 CDRW Mon, 8th Oct '12 12:25:59 PM
@ Crowlover: Yes! Those are what I was talking about.

It's not just german really. Those sounds, and the related ones that are found in several german and english related languages combine both the harsh s sound and the gutteralness of speaking from the back of the throat. Those sounds in many ways are to speaking what dissonance is to music. They make a language sound harsh (from the hissing), strong (from the gutteral sounds), and barbaric (possibly from the fact that English used to contain those sounds, and they bring up a cultural memory of more violent times).

Russian and German are about equal in perception. Russian might win out a little bit because of America's more recent enmity towards Russia.

Out of all the languages I've sang in, I've found German to be both the most difficult, and the most pleasing when done right. A huge part of singing is pronouncing each and every word correctly and clearly, never slurring the hard consonant sounds together. German has so many of those sounds that it takes a while to get acclimated to, and you have to focus on each and every word to make sure you don't slip. When you do get it right, though, well-written German lyrics practically come with their own percussion accompaniment thanks to that sound.
