#394580 Japanese Teeth Fri, 30th Aug '13 12:21:09 PM from Meinong's jungle
It was a no-win situation, really. It was either "do diverse casting and risk pissing off fanbase via perceived Race Lift" or "just make them all white and get in trouble for the lack of diversity". There are going to be Unfortunate Implications either way. I mean, the alternative is to either cast the characters at random and risk storms of people going "why is (X) (nationality)?" or attempt to match the races to their personalities, which is even more risky. They just went the route that will PO the fewest people. And arguably makes the most sense, as pony culture generally appears to be pretty European and all the characters are voiced by (and sound like) Caucasians anyway.

Basically, no matter what casting decision they made, someone was going to get mad about it, so they just went with the least risky option. Of course that begs the question of why they made it in the first place, knowing that someone would raise a stink.
