The Dresden Fillies is the more light-hearted of the two best-known MLP/Dresden Files crossovers, isn't it?
Well, I've only read the short story from MLD, and it was pure crack. The Dresden Fillies actually has a storyline and while it's rather dark and serious, it has quite a nice sprinkling of humour and waff to balance it out. It definitely isn't much worse than Canterlot Wedding. It's still much nicer than the books.

The sequel is being currently written, and still incomplete. It has Bob in it, and he is his smartass lecherous self. Expect a lot of dirty jokes from him. As for the story itself, it's a mixed bag. While nobody has been seriously hurt so far, I find it much creepier than the relatively straightforward first volume. By that I mean it moves from a typical D&D game to Paranoia. Ok, maybe that's an unfair comparison, but you get the point.

The point of this little tirade is, if you can accept a pony story that tries a more serious tone, with clashing moral codes and well intentioned extremists, while still retaining the funny and adorable of the source material, jump right in.
