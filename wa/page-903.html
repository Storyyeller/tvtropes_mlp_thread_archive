See dude, that's not exactly how this "Power Levels" thing works. By this reasoning the Vic Viper is stronger than the entire Bacterion army because it constantly defeat them, but it's kinda hard to argue that the ship that always dies in one hit is more powerful than the Big Core that can take about ten lasers before going down.

There's a lot more that comes into play in combative scenarios than just "who is stronger." The Diamond Dogs managed to hold off the mane cast for so long because they had the homefront advantage—they knew the territory, while the mane cast were having to deal with a challenge they had simply never faced before. And I think we need to cut Nightmare Moon some slack here. Keep in mind she's been locked up for 1000 years. Imagine if you're the best Street Fighter II player in the world, you have the world's best custom-built arcade stick and you can perform every move and combo at heart, and then you don't play it for a thousand years and then on the day of your release you're immediately thrust into a tournament. #22566 Enlong Mon, 26th Sep '11 8:07:17 AM from The Magitek Holyland from The Magitek Holyland
Or she's an experienced farm-hand and knows how to guide herds as a result.

The more I think about it, the more I like the idea that Nightmare Moon only looked weak in comparison because she preferred to do things subtly. Each of her tricks, with the exception of the Shadowbolt ruse, took as little effort on her part as possible. Why attack them herself, when she can enrage the first monster in their path? Why block their path herself, when a quick cut will get a sea serpent to do the job for her? Why conjure actual monsters, when she could just play off their fear of the forest itself? Why bring down lightning and such, when a simple shove can bring down the cliff they're on?

Really, I think what happened is she sorely underestimated them. When they actually reached the castle, that's when her hand was forced, and she simply teleported the Elements away and stamped them to pieces. Then she thought she'd won. Even when the necklaces appeared, she didn't think they'd get the sixth Element, and then it was too late for her to do anything, however powerful she was.

...and that would actually be a nice parallel. Both the major villains would have been defeated by their own overconfidence. It's just more of a blatant failing in Discord's case. Like everything else about him.
