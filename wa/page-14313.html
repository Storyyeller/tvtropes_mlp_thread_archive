Saddle Arabia in my fanon is an offshoot of El'bia, an earth-pony only country. After the 'death' of their first god, Amos of the shifting sands(Not to mention the related political issues), the country underwent a small schism and a small faction seceded and declared itself independent, taking on a couple 'ambitious' small-time old souls as their patrons. Part of Celestia making base with them was to keep the politics of the region stable(As Equestria was already a trading partner with El'bia), and partly to fully integrate the new gods into the cosmic council... and keep an eye on them. They're the youngest gods after Celestia and Luna, so they seemed to be the most likely to keep them calm, as opposed to say the roughly-50-thousand year old Ciel of the Sky and her extended family, or the moderately younger but rather imposing duo from their neighboring country, Ferghus of Iron and Simon of Stone.

@Sereg: Oh. Hm. Umm...

Cadence may be a goddess, she isn't technically a part of the cosmic council yet. She'll be officially included at the same time as the Saddle Arabian Delegates. For the moment they're just letting her ease into the Crystal kingdom, and they'll see if it recovers enough to become a new country, and therefore she'd be deemed a Regal Old Soul and made a part of the council, or if it remains a city-state, In which case previously she would have been moved to take control of El'bia as it's new leader, though there's a fraction of the council that desires to respect Amos's last wishes and not have a new council member take the throne, and Celestia would push for Twilight to take over after a while to keep Cadence from having to relocate, since the Crystal Ponies view her as a hero.

So she doesn't 'count' because she's not in control of a country and therefore still on the same level as basic old souls - who are not necessarily lesser than the council. The strongest Old Soul in existence generally wants nothing to do with the council, and the feeling is mostly mutual.

(Also, considering Luna and Celestia as the youngest doesn't necessarily make them the bottom, but they're far from the top - it was a narrative device used to demonstrate that there are ranks on the totem pole much higher and more dangerous than them.)
