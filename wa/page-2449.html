As far as the quality of the show goes, I try not to think about it too hard. Not to say that analysis is bad (hey, so far I've written about 60+ pages of review material for season 2), but you just have to remember that nothing is perfect, and that just because something has flaws doesn't mean that it's not still entertaining. I also try to keep Bellasarios Maxim in mind. I mean, you really can't be too hard on flaws like a lack of hard continuity in character development when it's specifically noted by the people who made it that they were instructed to make the episodes viewable in any order. I think the show as a whole is a lot better if you take it on an episode-by-episode basis. It just wasn't written with cumulative viewing in mind. Yeah, there are interconnections, but it's meant to be watched by people who probably have either not seen the other episodes, or don't really remember all the developments.
