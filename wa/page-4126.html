#103134 Kuroi Tsubasa Tenshi Tue, 21st Feb '12 9:58:37 AM from Canada
Ace: You could do that, but I know few plushie makers out there will put in wiring or skeletons because it reduces the cuddle-ability. I've got a 37" tall Fluttershy that didn't require any of that stuff and while she has trouble standing (mostly due to her ridiculous nearly 10 lb. weight), she looks just as cute sitting on her haunches (and so very cuddle-able). I would also recommend against doing a 3-4" plushie, especially if you want to have any kind of detail on it (I guess unless you're good with embroidery). My 8" Twi was an incredible pain to detail and have it not look sloppy (and she did still kind of turn out sloppy). I ended up having to cut out the smaller starbursts on her cutie mark. From what I've been told, going to either extreme makes it tougher (tiny plushies suck to detail and giant plushies have weight considerations), so middling sizes (seems to be anywhere from 12-19") tend to be favoured.
