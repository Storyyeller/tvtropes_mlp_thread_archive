My dad watched it with me (not a second time, the first time I watched it) and he thought up a Fridge Logic question. Basically, "Why didn't they just make the cake on the site of the contest like in real-life baking contests?" This was also because he pointed out that the train lurching around and stopping and starting could easily just dump over a creation as fragile as the MMM. In honor of the Sherlock Holmes parody in the story, I thought up both Watsonian and Doylist explanations for that:

Doylist: Because then they couldn't do the "Murder on the Orient Express" parody properly if the incident didn't happen on a train. (that was the answer I most easily thought of for his question)

Watsonian: Perhaps that particular contest was just for judging the entries and didn't have the supplies on hand, so participants had to bring the entries pre-made. Also, trains in Equestria might run smoother than real-life steam trains.

I watch the show with my dad sometimes, but I particularly wanted to show him this story because he LOVES trains and so I thought he'd like a story where almost everything happens on a train.
