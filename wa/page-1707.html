#42666 CDRW Mon, 21st Nov '11 11:34:36 AM
Touhou is a fandom that keeps to itself. But make no mistake, it is huge.

The amount of fanmade remixes for the music measures in the terabytes. The amount of art is even more. Touhou circles make up a huge percentage of doujin circles at anime conventions. And it is crossed over with absolutely everything. Danbooru is a great place to get a sense of just how much fan-made content there is. Or you could just take a look at the size of the Touhou thread in our own forum.

Also, if you come across an anime-style piece of art that involves a character you don't know, there's an even chance that it's Touhou.

#42673 Ace Of Scarabs Mon, 21st Nov '11 11:40:56 AM from Singapore from Singapore
Touhou encompasses many things.

Like Friendship Is Magic, most of the cast are girls with interesting designs and sometimes-interesting personalities. The music is excellent, easily the best synthesizer/chiptune goodness I have enjoyed. The fantastic realm of Gensokyo is a lush, pseudo-utopian place that can be dangerous like Everfree if you are careless. The plots of the games are simple, and everything usually ends with tea parties at Reimu's shrine. Gensokyo itself teems with fantastic life, taken mainly from Asian and European mythologies rather than from Gary Gygax's D&D manuals. It even crosses over with everything else at a rate that ponies cannot match just yet.

It may not be everyone's taste but I still like it.
