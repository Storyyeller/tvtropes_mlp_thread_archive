#112769 Kuroi Tsubasa Tenshi Fri, 2nd Mar '12 11:50:39 AM from Canada
I used to play MTG a lot, but I haven't in a while. I tried to get back into it a bit about a year and a bit ago, but I disagreed with some of Wizards' more inane rule changes, which, combined with removal being so powerful, killed the fun again pretty quickly for me. I had a Sliver deck, myself, although I wasn't actively trying to get the more expensive Slivers, so it was a pretty meh deck. Aside from that, I had 4 decks that were tied for my favourite:
  • Legendary deck - The concept behind this was more rule of cool, but I still managed optimize it into being usable against most serious (but non-tourny) decks. It basically became a green deck with multi colour creatures of a mix of all 5 colours that relied heavily on delay and land fetch tactics.
  • Angel deck - I expected this to be kind of sluggish like my Legendary deck (a very small amount of land fetch here in ye olde broken Land Tax, but some lower cost creatures were involved), but it was actually surprisingly sustainable. Probably due to the large amount of protection from X spells involved.
  • Pestilence deck - Cemetary Gates for self pro-black, nuke the field with Pestilence. I controlled the field at the cost of nuking myself. Although that could end up changing if the Urza's Armor came out (-1 damage from all sources and since Pestilence dealt damage 1 at a time...).
  • Wizard deck - A mishmash of older and newer wizards, bounces, counterspells and renewable card drawing. People got really frustrated playing against this deck, so I ended up benching it.

Er, wow, that was a lot of off-topic. Uh, so for on topic: Parasprites as a card would totally use the Devour mechanic. >.>
