two, will people let up on the fanfics having tiny little errors, yes we know they are not on par with something that's been through a publisher and editor, but who cares, the fanfic writers aren't trying to make money, it costs you nothing to read it, they are doing it for FUN, and so there readers can enjoy the story, stop trying to criticise them for not being the same quality as a novel that has gone though several rewrites, at least one of not more editors, etc... can the writers always improve, yes. But trying to point out these little things like they are glaring flaws gets old.

I'm making the assumption that fanfic writers (at least, fanfic writers that have long, epic stories with thousands of readers) probably want to improve, even if they have no intention of ever publishing anything. Small mistakes are not a big deal and don't detract much from the enjoyment of reading... but they are also very easy to fix once the writer knows what to look for and how to fix them. Seeing mistakes briefly jars the reader out of their engagement, so eliminating all the little mistakes makes for an uninterrupted, smoother read. It's not necessary but it does improve quality.

Even though I don't care for fanfiction myself, I do very much respect those writers who've managed to write stories that can hold so many people's attention. But I fail to see how wanting to help someone improve at their talent can possibly be offensive.
