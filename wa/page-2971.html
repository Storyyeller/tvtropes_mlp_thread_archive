#74268 Moe Dantes Thu, 19th Jan '12 12:34:56 AM from the Land of Classics
Yeah, there's that. Specifically, the whole motif of a Superhero story is that they're the few powerful dudes in a setting otherwise full of normals who are just like you and me, most exemplified by the original Superman comics of the 1930s which mostly focused on how his superhuman powers allowed him to overcome hazards that were a fact of life back then.

That kind of thing, in general, stops working well once the universe its set in gets more fantastic—if anything because there's little difference between a superhero and a guy like Conan or Bilbo Baggins in the first place.

In MLP, just about everyone has abilities, or at least quirks of character, that in Metropolis would be considered a superpower. Think of how many secret security vaults Pinkie Pie could break through with her power of offscreen teleportation. So basically the only thing to separate Mare-do-Well from anypony else is just that she wears a costume to hide her identity, which I always thought was one of the dumbest aspects of traditional superheroes, and it makes even less sense here since supposedly they're doing it just to teach Rainbow Dash a lesson in humility. How the hell this is supposed to do that, is anypony's guess. Then again, we are dealing with six characters who are quantifyably insane, so who knows what they were thinking.
