Even though it isn't actually all that grim, its kind of amusing to imagine that it is so, we just are treated to the good parts and the bad parts are hidden just out of sight. For instance, here's why I think Spike is a slave: the ponies have had him since he was an egg - which implies that they took him in before he was old enough to be able to make the decision whether or not he wanted to live with the ponies. Obviously I am choosing the darkest explanation here - maybe Spike is paid, maybe he was an orphan, maybe dragons can't hatch unless a unicorn casts a spell on their eggs - but hey, I like the world a bit more when its a little dark, so its my prerogative to pick up on these things and run with it .

Other stuff:
  • Twilight Sparkle is actually spying on the town of Ponyville for the Queen, and the Queen is using the information conveyed through the letters for some clandestine purpose.
  • The ponies have been shown to be aggressive colonizers with little regard for the needs of the natives. What is happening in Applepolooza is likely happening all over Equestria, with tragic consequences for any natives who are driven out of their lands.
  • The ponies make it a point to drive out any and all potential competitors from their realm. Dragon shows up, the ponies are dispatched to drive it out. Cockatrice on the loose? Not after Fluttershy hits it with The Stare. So what happens to that poor cockatrice when it cannot defend itself from bigger, nastier predators with its stone magic because Fluttershy took away its ability to do so with her Hypno Ray? And what about the countless other monsters that are basically told to get lost? Can they find proper food in their new habitats?
