#168581 Bluespade Sun, 22nd Apr '12 4:30:56 PM from Texas
Alright, elaboration a go: I'm gonna pretty much quote myself from last night. A lot of sci-fi/fantasy works tend to deal in a whole lot of exposition and info-dumps to characterize their settings. This tends to put the focus on the world while not putting enough focus on the characters and plot, creating a stale, inconsistent story. Other works, like MLP:FIM, develop their setting purely through direct evidence, relying on the viewers to infer further info about the setting. This both causes the viewers to become more engaged in the story and allows their imagination to fill in any accidental plot-holes, resulting in an over all better defined setting.

So basically, I don't think the show should ever pause the story and explain to us what it means to be a princess and where alicorns come from. We know what they are, we know what they do, that's all we need. We can fill in the rest ourselves.
