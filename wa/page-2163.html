#54071 kegisak Wed, 14th Dec '11 4:21:41 PM
I'm of the 'Trixi has an inferiority complex' camp as well, myself. I like to think that she had a 'friend [spoiler: read, bully]' who used to show her up or something so she has to prove that she's the best just to feel like she's any good. And possibly that because of her 'competition [spoiler: read, abuse]' with this 'friend' she's come to think that being able to humiliate = being good, in the way that a victim of physical bullying could easily come to believe that being able to beat somebody up = being strong.

I think that's what makes her so popular - it's so heavy in the show that it's effectively canon that she's insecure, but the vagueness of it makes it really easy to project onto her, and sympathize with her. I was picked on as a kid so I go for the bullying approach because it's what I understand. People give her their own problems so they have someone they can share them with, and sympathize with. They want to have someone they can share their problems with... and there's Trixie.

In a way I think I almost don't want her to come back - why I don't want any of the one off villains to come back. They all work as templates to project ourselves on to, because their always sympathetic. The audience wans someone to share their problems with, and there they are. The mane cast are good to be happy with, but they don't have problems. the villains are good to be sad with, because they do.
