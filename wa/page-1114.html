#27848 kegisak Fri, 14th Oct '11 12:11:22 PM
As a matter of opposites attract, methinks. The mythos of 40K is basically that everyone is at war with everyone, all the time. Every faction is made up of bastards of a different colour (except the orks, who are just having fun), and the whole thing is amazingly bloody and dark. So given the fandom's proprensity for dark fics, I'm sure you can see the draw.

As for the game itself, its methods mirror its mythos colosely. Its a game for bastards - victory largely depends on which player has the most cash to burn (and can therefore buy the more expensie, more retardedly powerful units), and who's willing and able to exploit loopholes the most. It also has the draw of actually replicating a battle, as the matches always consist of dozens of little pices all moving around, and the rules take actualnumerical distances into account when attacking. I've never played it myself, but I've watched a game, and let me tell you there's something strangely alluring about two giant men screaming at each other and dropping fistfulls of dice onto the table - up to 20 or so at a time, in one case.
