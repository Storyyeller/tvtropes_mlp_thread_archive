#349259 Psi 001 Wed, 20th Mar '13 7:16:05 PM
Try making a basic script of your idea first. Show it to some friends you trust (not ones that will either make fun or just say how awesome it is regardless) and ask for their opinions. If it passes, get started, if they don't like it, ask for specific criticisms and improve accordingly. Preferably give to a range of people with differing preferences so as to get opinions from different view points.

I remember thinking the same when I first thought of making a fan comic, I lingered over it for almost a year, I made a test page, and now I've pretty much taken up all my spare time working on finishing the project. I'd only not advise it on the grounds it may similarly overwelm your entire life.

#349266 ATC Wed, 20th Mar '13 7:29:14 PM
Most comic strips have some similar formats; and a lot of them rely on the second-to-last panel being the "beat" panel; or the last panel revealing something different than expected

Example Comic:

First Panel:

  • Boy: Oh, I missed you so much, my darling

Second Panel:

  • Girl: I'm sorry I was away for so long

Third panel:

  • Boy: I hope I never have to spend so much time without you ever again. I love you! You bring so much joy into my life.

Fourth Panel:
  • Girl: I can't wait to hug you and squeeze you and snuggle with you

Fifth panel: -zoom out, revealing that the boy is talking to his laptop; and the girl is talking to her teddy bear-

Most of the strip is setup for the final revelation.
