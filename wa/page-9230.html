#230750 kegisak Thu, 21st Jun '12 4:35:54 PM
@Pannic: Hm. Yeah, I can kind of see your issue with that. I mean on the one hand, it's not a huge stretch for a pacifist to agree to only being an accessory - they'd still object of course, but it wouldn't be too hard to swing them around in the end if it were really the only option. The trick here is the emotional aspect, because no matter whether or not they agreed and no matter whether or not it was necessary, it seems like they'd NOT be happy with it.

So if Velvet really does just switch to musing about playing dress-up, that's bad characterization. and I don't believe you can blame the viewpoint in this situation. If the audience views what appears to be bad characterization, due to viewpoint or otherwise, that is a failure as a writer. Pip would, in this situation, be directly facing Velvet. Moreover, she'd be on a drug that would increase her observational abilities. The author can describe gallons of emotion through body language alone, and if they miss that opportunity it is a staggering flaw.
