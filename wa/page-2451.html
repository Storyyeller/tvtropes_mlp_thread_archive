#61274 Japanese Teeth Tue, 27th Dec '11 11:04:50 PM from Meinong's jungle
I'm not necessarily saying that it should be held to lower standards, just that it should be held to different standards according to the purpose of the show. So MLP can be held to high standards as a children's show because that's what it is. Holding it to criteria that isn't appropriate to its' genre is going to result in the show being judged unfairly. Take the whole "continuity" issue for example; I find that a lot of (not all) complaints about the Negative Continuity of the show are unfounded because it was never supposed to have continuity to begin with. The show is designed to be taken by an episode-to-episode basis; it was never intended for all the episodes to build on each other. So when people complain about, say, the fact that there isn't a steady build-up of characterization as the season progresses, I can't fully agree with it because that was never the show's goal. It's consistent (the episodes don't contradict each other), but not cumulative (they don't build on each other as the season progresses).
