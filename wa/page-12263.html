#306572 Japanese Teeth Wed, 21st Nov '12 9:39:06 PM from Meinong's jungle
They're comparable, but I don't know if I'd go that far. Party of One was good in that it explored Pinkie's self-identity through a more mundane set of circumstances. It took the dominant aspect of her personality and basically deconstructed it by showing what happens when she loses it, and why. TMPP does something similar; it takes Pinkie's surface personality and picks it apart by showing that there's more too her than that.

But I think what causes Party of One to edge out ahead for me is the fact that in that episode, the conflict is entirely character based rather than requiring an outside trigger to kick off her breakdown. It developed more naturally in conjunction with the other characters as opposed to TMPP which largely had Pinkie hatch the scheme entirely on her own. Party of One had a bit more conflict between Pinkie Pie and her friends, whereas TMPP has the conflict almost entirely internalized.
