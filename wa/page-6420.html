@Seraph: What I mean is, I think the fandom as a whole has a bit of a front that it puts up, perhaps because of how it USED to be. I hopped on the wagon when it was still relatively small; Eq D made maybe a post or two per day, and they were mostly madmax's comics with the odd fanfiction. Derpy was occasionally portrayed as just a normal girl with a funny eye, and the peoples in the fandom genuinely followed the 'love and tolerance' mantra. It was a small amount of very reasonable people who got into it because they were reasonable enough to try it on their own, and it showed. then as the fandom started to get larger, more people were drawn in from... different walks of life. The 'love and tolerance' mantra became an "I'm better than you" badge, and the phrase began to lose it's meaning. In fact, the phrase itself is sometimes even used as a reason not to do so - WE love and tolerate, so we're better than them and can swear at them, cuss them out, and make fun of them. I've seen bronies almost universally bash different groups - furries, other fandoms, people who don't watch the show, hasbro, even each other. It's become wildly divisive in a lot of ways, and compared to what it USED to be it honestly depresses me a tad.

I mean, there are parts of it that are still good. When the people like things, they like them. It's a good fandom for producers, but as a community... I don't want any part of the community. Give me what fans I have, and a few good friends. The rest can stay away.
