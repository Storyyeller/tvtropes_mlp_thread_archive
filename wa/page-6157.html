@Starswirl: I recall reading something similar to that, actually. In Hogfather, there's a character who can do such things as walking through walls - but only when she doesn't think about it. If she's aware that she's going to hit a wall, then she hits it.

The reason given is basically that she doesn't have any reason not to keep moving, and so the ambient magic reacts to that. Maybe Pinkie's cartoonish abilities work on the same principle - EP's may actually be more fundamentally connected to magic than other pony breeds, and so if they feel that they should be able to do something, they can.

Also, just got back from playing a binge of Persona4. It's really, really good. Like, I get the feeling I'm not even out of the tutorial phase, but I love it so far. The story ferrying still feels like good gameplay and all the characters are really fun and engaging. Like, aside from your homeroom teacher I genuinely like them all. The gameplay, when you get there, feels really good and solid. The design of the worlds and enemies is interesting, and the fight sections feel really quick, powerful and just plain good. I honestly kind of like your allies auto-attack, though I'll still be acting manually for the most part as it simply makes it easier to react to cues. Not to mention that soundtrack! The theme is incredibly tight and pervasive, even when the emotional parts come early I still care, and everything is so amazingly designed, right down to the UI. It just feels so good! It's not quite journey levels of good, but from what it looks like so far it's still easily one of the best games I've played in a long damn time.

anyways, enough gushing. time for me to head to bed. Night, all.
