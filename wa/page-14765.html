#369113 kegisak Wed, 22nd May '13 4:29:48 PM
That depends entirely on how you want to spend your time. Fan projects and side projects like this can often end without anything ever having been done if people aren't dedicated enough. With something so very long term, something you aren't likely to see much personal gain from aside from your skill as an artist, tends to sap motivation pretty easily. Whenever I consider something like this I do it with a hefty dose of salt, bearing in mind - however cynical it may seem - that the odds are against it ever finishing.

So, the question to ask is, are you willing to put in a lot of time and effort on a project that probably will never finish, if it does may not become popular, and if it does you probably won't get much recognition for on a personal level, based solely on the chance to work and improve?

It's a dour way to look at things, I'll grant you, but on the flip side if you do make it, and it does get done, the sense of pride from something as simple as being able to look at this and say, "Yes, I made this. This is mine" is mindboggling.
