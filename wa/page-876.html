Your premise here is flawed. Fiction is a lens through which we see an isolated and magnified part of some people's lives, usually the parts that are most interesting. All the things we love about Sherlock Holmes are in him solving mysteries, which is why we never hear a story which revolves around him running a winery or anything like that—it just wouldn't be interesting. On the other hand, Rarity is a character whose (arguably) best showing revolved around her making dresses. Obviously, such mundane plots are better for a character like her, and what logical process would lead someone to conclude that being good with a needle makes her perfect for fighting supervillains? Exactly. It kinda works in episodes like Dragonshy which are diplomacy missions though.

It odd that you prefer Slice of Life and most of fan stuff is more adventure style.

Admittedly I've noticed that contradiction too. But the thing is, fan stuff is fan stuff. Fans are free of many constraints that bog down the TV show (they don't have to deal with censors, they don't have to constrain themselves to a time limit, and they can make big sweeping epic changes since it doesn't affect the core canon), so its arguably more acceptable there.
