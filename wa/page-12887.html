Also, what's tier 3?

In D&D 3.5ed, classes are commonly rated in tiers from 1 to 6 on the basis of power and versatility. Tier 3 is generally considered the sweetspot since they don't break the game like a Tier 1 (Wizard, Cleric) and aren't completely useless like a Tier 5 (Fighter, Monk). Though it doesn't really matter as long as everyone in the party is on roughly the same tier.

Despite the traditional derision for bards and jokes about how they are useless, they are actually tier 3, making them more powerful or versatile than many of the traditional classes including Rouge, Barbarian, Ranger, Fighter, Monk, and Paladin.
