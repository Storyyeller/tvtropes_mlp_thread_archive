#163685 kegisak Fri, 20th Apr '12 2:48:21 PM
@Story: Well, I had to develop a completely unique culture for Aloa when I wrote TCYB. The thing is, I never developed the full culture. Sure, I've got the basics of it down - they're neighborly, proud, value community etc., as well as some minor details like condoning slavery and the use of medallions as identification and the wrappings as a sign of status(It was never outright stated, but generally the more heavily a pony wraps themselves is a sign of how much money they have), but I never went into depth about all the minutia. And I think that's the trick.

Basically, give your readers just a little snippet of culture here and there, and it'll feel foreign enough for them to settle. Of course, that's assuming you have to invent a culture like I did...
