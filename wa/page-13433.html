#335812 Japanese Teeth Tue, 5th Feb '13 1:48:22 PM from Meinong's jungle
Yeah, I'm guessing it was a combination of factors that resulted in them deciding to cut it a bit short.

Also, regarding comparative quality, I remember seeing a comic somewhere that broke down the most common tendencies in shows with multiple seasons. If memory serves, it went something like:

1. Decent; writers are still getting a grasp on the material.

2-4. Typically the strongest seasons; the writers are familiar with the characters, and the material is still fresh enough that they have consistent quality.

5-6. Quality begins to decline; writers get fatigued/leave/start running out of ideas. Still not bad; there are still good episodes in there, but the overall quality declines.

7-whatever: Franchise zombie stage.

Obviously not every show follows that, but the general trend is there.
