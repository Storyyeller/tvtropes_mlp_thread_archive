My theory on the weather team is that most of their job is 'scouting', with various other duties and levels of intensity based on altitude. The high-altitude team is long-range scouting, they look at the clouds and the wind currents in the high altitudes and use it to predict what weather patterns are forming naturally. That helps the mid and low altitude teams to get prepared. The mid-altitude team(Where Dash works) ensures that the weather patterns are along the scheduled routes - so while it looks like they've got a lot of free time, they do have to be on call pretty much all the time in case the weather starts to stray from the course. The low-altitude team ensures that things on the ground are prepped for the oncoming weather, and will serve as messengers to let the other teams know if things are going wonky.
