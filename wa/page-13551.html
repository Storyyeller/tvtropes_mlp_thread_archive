@Applelight: Introversion/Extroversion isn't necessarily about confidence, it's more about whether or not one recharges by being alone or by being with others, and often whether or not one prefers being in groups to one-on-one interactions. #338774 Rainbow Wed, 13th Feb '13 10:10:50 AM
Being introverted doesn't mean that one never likes to interact with others, just like being extroverted doesn't mean that one never likes to have any alone time, it's on a continuum. Twilight may still like to interact with her closest friends and may have fun when she's with them, but she also definitely seems to need her alone time more than some of the others do. As I mentioned before, when introverts want to interact with others, it's usually in a one-on-one situation rather than in a big group, or with people they are already close to if it's a bigger group, like with Twilight and the others in her group. If she was more extroverted she would probably be more interested in expanding her group of friends to include more of Ponyville.

I suppose some of this is because I'm an introvert myself and I'm more comfortable with addressing a crowd where I can control the interaction (like giving a speech or performing a song) than with actually interacting with people I'm not close to. I suppose to me it's about the level of control I have over the interaction, to me, giving a speech or talking AT people rather than WITH them is a different kind of interaction than the give-and-take of being in the crowd (it also helps that if I'm the one performing, I can tell everyone else to be quiet and listen to ME). Much of this may simply be my Asperger's rather than simply introversion, though. Twilight seems kind of like that type to me, like when she announces things to a crowd like in Just in Time.
