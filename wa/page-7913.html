@Moe, Ginger: While it's certainly not a hard and fast rule, it's not without merit. Any genre or story that has a specific villain, the villain is often the impetus to the plot. Of course at the same time they can just as often be an obstacle for the protagonist's goal, meaning the protagonist was the impetus and the antagonist isn't driving the plot.

It doesn't make the villains less important, but it's good to keep in mind that there are genres that can, and often do, do without.
