And only one thing stops it from turning into a total disaster area. Chief Thornhoove's best policeponies. Two deadbeat cops who get the job done, no matter what. If there's a crime to be had, they'll find the crooks. They're the best. They're Beat and Donut. And they're currently on break.

Beat flicked the Equestrian Daily paper aside, watching Donut gleefully hug her coffee cup. Cursed filly. Only time she was ever happy, coffee breaks. Not that they hadn't had their fare share lately. Things had been quiet. Too quiet. Almost as if the narrative opening was starting to be wrong, that they were starting to actually clean up this town. No sooner had the thought entered his head, his radio crackled and Thornhoove's voice assaulted his ears. He watched Donut twitch as well, coffee cup falling from her grasp. "GET TO THE CORNER OF FILLY SQUARE! A BAKERY ON PINE STREET WAS JUST ROBBED! THE PIE THIEF STRIKES AGAIN! BREAK TIME'S OVER, SLACKERS!"

Sighing as the ringing died away, Beat rose and pulled Donut away from the shattered cup. "Come on, come on, before he yells again." They dashed out of the small coffee shop, Donut pulling down her helmet and taking wing above the crowded street, flicking the whirling blue and red light on as she yelled the tone to warn everypony to move and make way, Beat barreling along beneath her. Another assignment, another day. Beat grinned.
