Re: fics: I agree with Blue.

By publishing their work on the Internet for other people to read, it opens them up to scrutinization. Enjoyment does often hinge one how well written a story is because, well, writing is the sole way the story is being communicated (with some exceptions, I guess, but even the ones that use pictures rely more on words than the pictures). Just because someone can overlook a little mistake doesn't mean they didn't notice it and have the flow disrupted. Just because the mistake doesn't bother one person, it might bother the next one. I've read a few of the shorter 6 stars (Cross and Arrow and My Little Dashie) and felt like they would have been a lot more enjoyable if more effort had to be put into revisions. Obviously since the fics are rated 6 star, there's far more people out there willing to overlook the mistakes than me.

#227563 Bluespade Mon, 18th Jun '12 3:00:32 PM from Texas
Fanfiction has a big disadvantage because it's expected to be written serially. A professional writer usually writes the whole draft first, then edits the entire thing several times before showing it to an editor, who then points out even more things that need to be rewritten, so by the time it's published it's been redone four or five times, resulting in MUCH higher quality.

Of course serial writing has some big advantages as well. You get constant feedback (well, you do if you have readers) and motivation to keep writing.

I do wonder how well received it would be if someone were to write a big epic story like CRISIS or FOE, rewrite and edit it several times, then post it online all at once, fully complete. It would definitely be something people would talk about, I think.
