#241740 Bluespade Tue, 3rd Jul '12 9:41:52 PM from Texas
Take a show like Family Guy, and there's a joke. The characters are being used as mouthpieces to tell the joke, mostly to set up a cut to a visual representation of said joke for a few brief seconds that has nothing to do with the rest of the episode. It doesn't matter which character is speaking. They don't have to have a reason to be talking. The characters are interchangeable. They have some characteristics, some traits of their own, sure, but when joke time comes around they are all just mouthpieces.

MLP avoids that almost entirely. The characters are almost always In Character, so the writers have to think of some way that makes sense for the characters to say something funny, which requires more clever thinking.
