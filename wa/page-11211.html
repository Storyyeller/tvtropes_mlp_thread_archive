#280265 Japanese Teeth Fri, 7th Sep '12 12:06:00 PM from Meinong's jungle
@Perpetual: I think what he means is that there are circumstances in the show in which point you'd expect that sort of reveal to come up. For example, I think the reveal that SA was Twilight's brother mostly worked because there were almost no points in the show in which it would have made sense to bring up that information; SA was almost never an active force in the story so there was no reason to introduce him.

Cheerilee and Scootaloo, on the other hand, interact on a regular enough basis that if they're sisters, it would almost certainly have been mentioned, and the fact that it wasn't would be a rather glaring omission. Case in point, if they're siblings, then it should have come up in Sisterhooves Social, or any point where Scootaloo and Cheerilee interact. H&H is the most obvious; as Apple Bloom mentioned that it was odd to pair Cheerilee up with her brother. If Scoots is related to Cheerilee, then it definitely should have been mentioned.

Or the idea of Scootaloo having a disability. She's been around long enough without it ever being mentioned that bringing it up now in the sense of "oh, didn't we mention that Scootaloo has always had gimp-wing" would just be weird. On the other hand, introducing it as a disability could work (e.g. Scoots gets fed up with her inability to fly and sees a doctor about it).

TL;DR:

  • Revealing new info about a character = fine

  • Revealing new info about a character that logically should have been mentioned earlier = not fine.
