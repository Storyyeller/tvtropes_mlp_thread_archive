One thing I really liked about this episode was that unlike other series that use this kinda premise, it never portrayed the machine or the technology as being bad or wrong. And they never quite showed hard work to be inherently inferior to the machine, either. The Apple Family was close to accepting a partnership and increasing their harvesting ability with the machine until the brothers revealed the horribly lopsided split, and they were able to compete handily with the machine simply by upping the number of workers from 4 to 8. I imagine that a family reunion would have left the machine in the dust.

I think I really liked that the real "moment of truth", so to speak, for both teams, was when they were each tempted to cut out quality control for the sake of speed. That turned it from being a question of "which method is better" to a question of integrity and pride in the quality of your work. And if there's anything Applejack is supposed to have in abundance, it's integrity and honor.
