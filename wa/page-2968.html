#74187 kegisak Wed, 18th Jan '12 7:30:14 PM
No, seriously. Sequential storytelling is just different from any sort of media. You don't show what's happening in real time, so you have to pick and choose what's important enough to show. Depending on how you do it you can manipulate how the reader feels the passage of time in the comic - like the link I posted, half of the panels are just Dumbbell's eyes moving. It makes every twitch that much more important, and extends the feeling of time to make what could have been a few seconds feel like a much more drawn out, and therefore important, event.

It's basically the Law of Conservation of Detail distilled and refined. You have to pick and choose what you show the reader in every single panel for maximum effect. Body language and expression is distilled to still shots instead of full motion, which limits how much of it the viewer can recognize and makes what you do show hugely important. Even something as simple as panel layout can drastically affect the feeling of a comic. It doesn't seem like it, but there's a lot more to comics than most people think, and it is, literally, a very different sort of thing than most people are used to critiquing.
