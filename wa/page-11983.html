#299567 Japanese Teeth Thu, 8th Nov '12 10:21:30 AM from Meinong's jungle
@Applelight: Going solely by the show's canon; they don't seem to have any form of organized religion. Their society is based around the central idea that friendship is awesome, but it isn't really religious in any notable way. The basic idea is compatible with both religious and secular philosophies, so you could really spin it either way. They obviously won't bring up the subject in-show, though.

Likewise, races or other countries might have different core philosophies (Again, we have no idea), and some of them might be formalized as religious in nature, but it's really entirely conjecture.

Honestly, I don't even necessarily buy that Celestia and Luna are even deities. The reverence they receive from the ponies borders on it at points, but there's no indication that they're anything other than ponies who happen to be powerful and long-lived. The only being who approaches the level of power of divinity is Discord, and even he got his butt kicked by the elements. Honestly, if anything the abstractions of the elements of harmony are the guiding forces of the setting. They're the only things that haven't been defeated in some fashion; every time they're used, they end up winning.

Also, That it is not at all what faith means in a religious context, at least not most of the time. It's something more akin to trusting someone or something. It's entirely possible for the ponies to have faith in Celestia; in fact, that's practically been shown. They have faith in the sense that they trust her to do the right thing despite the fact that it's impossible to quantifiably prove that she will. It's a character thing, not a "does this thing objectively exist or not exist" thing.
