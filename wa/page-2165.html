Everything that Trixie does can be attributed to lights and sounds. He cutie mark even has a wand on it - a commonly cited symbol of trickery when it comes to magic, or a booster. Not true magic, like Twilight's. Trixie's special talent is performance, and hence her magic revolves around illusions - lights and sounds. @Kegisak: Man, I wish I had three hours a night to spend on my writing. I usually get an hour at best.

@ginger: Once you get over that initial hump, it gets a little easier to write. And everybody's first writing is always crap. Some of the best advice about writing I've ever gotten is "assume that your first draft is going to suck". You're never going to get Shakespeare quality stuff right off the bat. That and the only way to get better at writing is to write. Yeah, the first few stories might not be all that great; but you'll learn how to make subsequent works better.

Also, the reasons one bad decision tends to stick more than one good decision is that the bad decision tends to have way worse consequences than a good decision, and that a act of good is way easier to fake than a bad one. A bad person will do a good thing to try to trick people; a good person obviously won't do the opposite.
