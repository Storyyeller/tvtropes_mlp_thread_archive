#195647 Psi 001 Tue, 15th May '12 7:03:15 PM
I'm not big on Villain Protagonists, not permanent ones anyway (A Day in the Limelight for a more traditional villain can work). I mean, I often like the characters themselves, but that's the problem. They are meant to be sympathetic and the Hero Antagonist isn't. While I like Sympathetic villains, I find it bittersweet when the guy you know will always beat them isn't rootable. It's one of the key things I have against shows like The Dreamstone, Grim And Evil and a lot of Looney Tunes shorts.

I often like hero characters but they often have a very specific quality for me to get into them. I often find there's a very thin line between a benevolent hero thwarting an evil villain and a smug jackass bullying a harmless bumbler. Maybe it's just a thing I have with Butt Monkeys.

Saw that film, it's an oldie with lots of dated upper crust dialogue but pretty good. Heard they edited it in the US because they didn't like the Villain Protagonist looking too sympathetic though.
