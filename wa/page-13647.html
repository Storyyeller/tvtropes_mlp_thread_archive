Most of the complaints are valid... but only if you agree with his headcanon on how cutie marks work, which almost no one here does. Basically, he's complaining that the show violated Magic A Is Magic A, while everyone else (I think Perpetual said it) pointed out that the spell was Magic B. It doesn't violate any established rules because there aren't established rules. We know how things generally work, but it's never said that they can't work that way. TL;DR most of his complaints boil down to "I think it works this way" even though in most cases we simply don't have enough information to lay down real rules about how magic works in the setting.

IMHO, time magic should be (very) limited in any setting.

I do like Doctor Who, watch it regularly, but time travel is UGH... I always end up hating myself every time I am compelled to apply any bit of logic to time travel.

Like with Harry Potter, I was (facepalm) when time magic showed up in this 'verse so I am very much glad that we only saw it used once (so far), just like in HP.

The thing is that most works just use time travel as a plot device to allow Time Period Of The Week or Dues Ex Machinas. Time travel breaks plots if you take it seriously, so the only way to have it make sense is if it is the entire point of the work, like with Primer or Achron.
