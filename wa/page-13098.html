My stance on Derpy; I would have preferred they stuck to the name Ditzy Doo (which Amy Rogers said was going to be the case until a last minute change for the sake of a more obvious Fandom Nod) as it was a name the writers came up with themselves (and not some guy on 4chan) and was already used for an established-as-but-not-seen-silly pony. I don't mind calling her Derpy though, and I'm sure the controversy probably would still have happened if they called her Bubbles or Grey Pony.

I do wonder if there was actually a Silent Majority complaining about Derpy that we never knew about. A bunch of angry parents/legitimately offended people going straight to Hasbro and never posted on Internet forums or starting up petitions? That always seemed more likely to me than the Vocal Minority of fans complaining how their precious Derpy turned out.

It amuses me, in a bizarre way, that Hasbro keeps finding ways to avoid calling her by name. Her Special Edition Toy, completely unnamed. The Gameloft game, not playable (unlike most of her BG peers) but appears in a way that doesn't require her to be named on screen. The comic series, gets a whole scene in the first issue but Twilight refers to her as "that pony", and gets a spider counterpart (the only spider to have dialogue, she/he? makes a Shout Out to The Far Side) in the second issue.

Look, Hasbro, I can accept if you never want to call her Derpy again, but call her something. Come up with a new name if you have too, bronies won't complain as much as you think they will. I think.
