Ahh, tabletop games and logical possibilities.

One of my friends is working out a system that uses very simple, but very wide-ranged effects that you can use in any way, so long as you can tie it to the ability you're using, and make a roll with difficulty decided by the DM.

For example, a Mage could have the spell "Resist". You could try Resist Cold to survive low temperatures. Resist Impact to have better armor in a fight. Resist Hunger to go without food. And so forth. Other spells like Flash or Beautify have interesting qualities if you put your mind to it. An then there's stuff like "Create [Fire/Earth/Ice/Wind/Food]".

Even physical classes have some interesting stuff. Say you're a Fighter. How many different ways can you interpret a move called "Smash"?
