@Dark: You'd be surprised. A lot of stories are good by virtue of how they handle the material rather than what the premise is. Yeah, pretty much every premise has been done in some way, but there are always ways to add to it. Nine times out of ten, execution trumps concept.

Also, there's that famous old adage: write what you know. Maybe you could write something from the POV of a pony who has difficulty seeing; that way the difficulties in describing the setting could be an asset to the story because you know firsthand what that's like. I'm guessing their aren't a lot of writers out there with firsthand experience of that, and I'm sure there are people out there who would find it interesting to have a story that focuses entirely on sensations other than visual description.
