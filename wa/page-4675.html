#116867 CDRW Tue, 6th Mar '12 1:01:55 PM
@ Ace: Those are too strong. Part of what makes a violin last is that the glue is weaker than the wood, so if you drop it or knock it, the glue seam will crack instead of the instrument. Then you can re-glue it and be good as new.

Also, they form non-polar bonds, which means they're stronger, but don't dissolve in water or alchohol. That means you can't easily take the instrument apart to do repairs or other work. Not only that, you can't re-glue without removing all the old glue (causing massive damage) because it won't stick to itself.
