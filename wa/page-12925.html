I think, Anarchist, you are perhaps looking at this a tad too doylistically. Consider it from a character viewpoint: Why did Spike not burn the Timberwolves? Well one, we don't know that that would work. They're made from enchanted wood, that shit could react funny. Second, Spike is a child. If he sees a pack of monsters running towards him, is his first reaction going to be to fight back? No, it's probably going to be to run, because he was frightened.

On the note of running, this is far from the first time we've seen the girls run fro ma monster, even ones they saw coming - this time, the Timberwolves got the drop on them. Saviors of equestria, sure, but the girls aren't warriors. We've seen them run away before. In fact the only times they've stood up and fought was when they had to, so we've no reason to assume their first reaction would be to fight if they thought they could get away. Hell, AJ dove right into the fray when she saw Spike in danger, and even then her strategy was to distract them from Spike so they could both get away. That's neither inconsistent nor misogynistic, that's demonstrated and realistic behavior for the girls.

As for Spike being clumsy, well, he's a personal assistant, not a labourer. He helps Twilight organize books, something that doesn't tend to involve lifting things several times larger than him - and on the occasions it does you might notice he tends to drop them too.
