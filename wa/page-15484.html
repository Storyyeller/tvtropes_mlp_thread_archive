However, I was a little reluctant at first to try out the new show because I was afraid it would be anti-intellectual and say that it's bad to be smart and studious because I read the plot summary of "Twilight is a studious, aloof unicorn who is told to stop studying so much and go make friends" but thankfully it never really went down the path I feared it would (Twlight's trait of being smart and studious isn't treated as a negative trait of hers most of the time and is often what helps her solve problems, like when she taught Pinkie how to analyze clues in the mystery train story). I also was wary of the idea that they made the "princess of the night" be the one who turns evil, but I read the spoilers (I'm a Spoiler Hound) and was reassured that Luna gets turned good again at the end of the pilot. The first episode I watched was Applebuck Season, and that's what got me into the show because the scene where Applejack and Pinkie make the baked bads reminded me of the kind of silly "characters mess up at cooking" stories I used to make up when I was younger.

I think I started watching the show between the first and second seasons, so the second season was the first one I watched as it aired.
