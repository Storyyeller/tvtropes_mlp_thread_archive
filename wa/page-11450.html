#286236 Perpetual Lurker Sat, 22nd Sep '12 9:56:13 PM
@Mousa The thing about Joseco, though is that what he draws is just good. The difference between him and you, though is that he doesn't experiment. People already like his style, and he likes his style, so why should he? You, on the other hand, are doing risky things. If you're going to react this badly when people react negatively to an odd style, then you shouldn't be doing such an odd style.

Think about why you're drawing. Are you drawing for yourself, or to get attention of others? If you're drawing for yourself, then why do you even care what people think about your art? If you're drawing because you want people to look at what you drew, then you're going to have to take a hint when people tell you what they do and don't like. Find what they do like and enhance it, and find what they don't like and tone it down. If you notice, none of the really unique choices of your style (the races, unique body types) are what people don't like. You can adapt to the wills of your audience without sacrificing your integrity and vision.

Dude, I know you dislike JJ, but he's not popular solely because he churns out lots of humanizations. He's also popular because he draws stuff like this on a regular basis. I get that the sameyness of the humanizations bugs you, but that's hardly the only thing the guy draws.

EDIT: Also this. His style doesn't evolve much, no, but that's because it really doesn't need to. Yes, his humanizations have an Only Six Faces thing going, but the fact that the show itself does the same thing proves that you don't need to give everyone unique body types to make it work. I do wish there was more variation, but on the whole his designs are pretty good, and his pony art is really good. The other thing is that he isn't just popular because of his quality, he's popular because he's drawn hundreds of pictures. Even if the art is only middling, if you put out that much stuff people are going to notice.

And like Perpetual said, it comes down to the fact that you're the artist, and if you don't want to pander to the majority, you don't have to. But at the same time, the fact that the majority doesn't share your taste in humanizations doesn't make them wrong either. If they're dicks about it, they're dicks, but just because they prefer JJ's designs to yours doesn't make them idiots.
