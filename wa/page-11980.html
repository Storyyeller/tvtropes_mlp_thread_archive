#299480 Pykrete Thu, 8th Nov '12 12:36:16 AM from Viridian Forest
So I just played Episode 1 of MLP: Online (which was, ironically enough, not online). The field encounters are a little irritatingly dense, though it's probably exacerbated beyond what the rest of the game would be like because the opening dungeon is dark-ass Everfree where you can't see anything because they're trying to be all creepy (they succeeded). Part of it was also that I didn't realize you could rotate the camera with right-mouse, so for the first few rooms I ran into stuff a lot. In any case, I feel like I overleveled on accident.

The combat is unusually flexible for as early on as it is. Looking at the talents, there seems to be a tremendous range of specialization for each character, and they're almost all really powerful. Like, "X attack never misses", or "Applejack becomes passively immune to daze", or "Twilight regenerates MP like crazy". You can also allocate Element points for minor stat boosts, and reset them for free at a save point, so you can basically crank a bit more out of any given role on demand.

I absolutely adore the writing. It reminds me of Mardek. Just the right amount of WTF all around. Actually, the plot reminds me of Mardek too, but whatever.

It also doesn't appear to have the glaring compatibility issues that make so many of the pony games unplayably laggy.
