#340109 Japanese Teeth Sat, 16th Feb '13 10:45:28 AM from Meinong's jungle
Nightmare Moon was largely the elements though. Basically, we can say that some spells take more effort than others, and the amount of effort required isn't necessary proportional to how powerful or complicated the spell is. If anything that kinda makes sense. Simple spells take more effort because you're brute-forcing the result. The better at magic you are, the less effort it takes because you know how to most efficiently get the result you want. That's why stuff like the wing spell in "Sonic Rainboom" took so much effort; Twilight had never used it before. In fact, most of the spells that Twilight is seen struggling with are ones that she hasn't studied or attempted before.

In this case, we have a spell written by a nigh-legendary unicorn (who'd know how to make it efficient) cast by another unicorn who's a magical prodigy (who would also have a good idea of hwo to cast it). If that's true, it really isn't surprising that the spell didn't look that hard.
