Okay, now that my mind is a lot cleaner, let me elaborate on my opinion on the Canterlot Wedding.

What I Didn't Like

  1. The whole sudden introduction. No matter how I try to look, it seems clear to me that Shining Armor and Cadence were created just for this episode. I am feeling as if I am forced to feel empathetic toward them.

  2. Chrysalis (I feel awkward for using that nickname) just isn't that interesting of a villain, especially compared to likes of Discord. She feels too much like just a generic villainess. To make it worse, her presence doesn't even feel threatening. I feel that she went down way too easily. I guess she had far too little screentime for that.

  3. The goddesses and the mane cast just got shafted in favor of Shining Armor and Cadence. Celestia went down far too easily (couldn't she have at least struggled in that coccoon?) and Luna is nowhere to be seen until the end. The mane cast was ultimately unable to do anything to tip the scale of the game. I personally wanted to see Luna pulling a Big Damn Heroes moment, but I guess that was too much.

  4. The entire atmosphere was far too corny to my taste, especially the song.

  5. It wasn't long enough. Maybe if it was, there would have more room for make things awesome.

This is about it at the moment.

Sorry to just hop in and blab something random(I know I've been doing that a lot recently, due to lack of free time with a job and all), but something's occurred to me the other day:

So when a cloud makes percipitation, due to altitude it's always snow first - it melts and becomes rain as it falls if the lower air is warm enough. This, of course, means that it's really cold up at cloud level. Basic weather, right? RD even says that upper altitudes get chilly in Dragonshy.

But here's the thing: It's very clearly NOT cold in Cloudsdale - The girls and the citizenship are abole to walk about without any clothes to speak of. You can make the case that Pegasi are naturally insulated, but the others? Plus, we see that the weather workers making snowflakes ARE dressed for the cold.

Which points to Cloudsdale being temperature-controlled, which is... rather significantly harder than weather control, I should think.

I wonder if they just settle on a year-round temperature, or if they have their own seasons? They're above the weather line, so there's really no reason for them to subject themselves to cold OR heat if they don't need to, so Cloudsdale could realistically be like, Paradise all year round.

I consider this because I'd been wondering how somepony born and raised in Cloudsdale would adapt to ground-life, and I got to thinking about how it might be really hot because of them being used to the cold upper atmosphere, but then.. well, all this.
