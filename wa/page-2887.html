#72162 Japanese Teeth Sun, 15th Jan '12 3:35:51 PM from Meinong's jungle
I don't know if I'd say flattest so much least repressed. Fluttershy basically wears her feelings on her sleeve. Pinkie is probably a simpler character, but the fact that she actively hides the insecure parts of her personality makes it easier to work her into conflicts. There's an element of uncertainty as to what's going on in Pinkie's mind (other than PARTYPARTYPARTY), whereas with Fluttershy what you see is what you get.

The other thing is that Pinkie is just a much more active personality; she can actively participate in the conflict. With Fluttershy, the conflicts are more limited to "Fluttershy gets stuck in a situation that she can't quite deal with" because she'd never deliberately get herself into a conflict with another pony, and if she did, she'd probably resolve it too fast to build an episode around it.
