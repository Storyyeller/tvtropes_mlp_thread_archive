As for villains ginger, my taste is very picky. I tend to prefer sympathetic comedic villains, however if they become too pitiful I start to lose empathy for the heroes or feel they are 'bullying' them, so having them surprise them with a Not So Harmless moment whenever it seems they're becoming too much of a laughing stock. Basically a balance of karma and sympathy, a villain that's menacing enough to be cheered against, but charming enough to be liked as a character. Beware the Silly Ones through and through.

The more I think about it, the more Discord fits this territory perfectly really.

#149568 Psi 001 Sat, 7th Apr '12 8:00:46 PM
Hero Antagonist characters tend to be dull for me. You always feel like you're missing something that could make the scenario a lot more complex.

A media with a normal hero can have the hero be the protagonist but still have an expanded and depthful universe as well as a sympathetic villain at times. A Villain Protagonist show on the other hand tends to skew everything so you get the villain is the sympathetic character and you don't root for anyone else.

It's a huge reason I think The Dreamstone was a waste of potential for example. The pilot had Rufus as the protagonist and utilized a more balanced wholesome development of it's universe similar to FIM. The show afterwards was about the Urpneys however so everything else was Out of Focus or drained of character. Akin to the previous analysis I also felt the Urpneys were too pitiful without the heroes getting proper Sympathetic P.O.V..
