I totally agree that Spike needs to be incorporated into the main cast more, which is why I really really hope this episode focuses at least a little on his relationships with the cast rather than isolating him the whole time. Both of his previous episodes had that issue; Spike didn't have much interaction. In "Owl's Well" he was alone most of the time, and in "Secret of my Excess" he wasn't exactly himself for most of the episode. Really, I think he was at his best in Lesson Zero because he had a function there; he was a foil to Twilight and help balance her character out rather than just being a plot device.

As far as Pinkie goes, I very much prefer it when her wackiness is primarily a product of her personality rather than her ability to bust the fourth wall or defy the laws of physics. That's why I think she was actually handled pretty well in "It's about Time", she was just as random as she always is, but aside from the Pinkie Sense appearing once, she didn't really do anything that the other ponies wouldn't be able to. "Eh, it's Pinkie" is a much better excuse when the weirdness is just her personality rather than her ignoring the rules of the setting.
