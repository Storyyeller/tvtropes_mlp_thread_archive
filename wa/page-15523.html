#388053 Japanese Teeth Mon, 5th Aug '13 9:19:07 AM from Meinong's jungle
I'm not disputing that they're awesome at what they do, I'm just saying that I personally don't find any of them so awesome apart from the ponies that I would be particularly worked up about meeting them. There's a huge difference between making a popular original work that inspires tons of fanartists, and the fanartists themselves who may very well be incredibly skilled, but are only popular because they're creating fanworks of something that gives them a built-in fanbase.

I don't see how it's any different then what the show staff do, other then the staff get payed for it, the fans simply sue to loving it.

I do think there is a bit of a difference here, namely that the people who created the show did so (mostly) from scratch and managed to be uber-popular, whereas most fanartist are only well known because they draw pony art.

Again, it's not at all that they aren't amazing artists and that they can't inspire others. It's just that I find the original work to be an order of a magnitude more impressive because it managed to be bone-crunchingly popular despite not having a built-in audience. Yes, the fanart may be amazing and inspiring, but I honestly don't think that 90% of pony artists would be half as popular as they are if they had never drawn any pony-related material. However skilled they are, they still owe their popularity to the show, whereas the people who made the show don't owe their popularity to anyone but themselves.

I'm not saying it's in any way bad to admire great fanartists, but at the same time I have far more admiration for those who can create something and popularize it without having to attaching to something that's already popular.
