#396302 Pykrete Sat, 7th Sep '13 2:44:53 PM from Viridian Forest
The appropriate use of fonts is something you actually get taught in technical writing and web development courses. Serif fonts like Times New Roman are designed specifically to ease reading of large reams of text. Sans-serif fonts (like this one) are best used for small, controlled blocks designed to be read quickly, such as smaller divisions on web pages or road signs. Courier and System (like in the TVT editing screen) are used for typing code, as they are formatted to constant width across characters. Certain other fonts like Impact are designed to evoke a businesslike feel by resembling older printing presses. Comic Sans, of course, is designed to evoke a whimsical feel that's rarely appropriate for what it's most often used for. And so on.

Fonts have a tremendous amount of cognitive baggage associated with them. You can't just throw whatever font you feel like on the screen.
