#41085 Rainbow Thu, 17th Nov '11 6:17:09 PM
@Ginger: I understand how you feel, even though I like Pinkie Pie, because I've felt that way before. I don't hate you at all, you more remind me of myself when I used to rant on forums about how I hated certain Sailor Moon characters. I can definitely understand how the show's treatment of Pinkie Pie can annoy some people.

Ironically, even though it stars my two favorites, I'm not that fond of "Feeling Pinkie Keen" because of the way it handled the moral. I know they weren't necessarily going for a religion-related moral but I don't really like that it implies that there are some things that shouldn't be studied. Or at least it could've been more explicit that Twilight's problem was less that she was trying to study Pinkie and more that she didn't want to admit that there might be forms of magic that she hasn't heard of yet. Pinkie Sense could definitely be studied scientifically (you could put Pinkie in situations that would make the twitches happen or at least record the twitches and then what happens afterward), but Twilight was more trying to prove that it wasn't real rather than being excited to learn something new. I guess I would've liked it better if it had been more that the moral was "real science is exploring new ideas, not stubbornly trying to prove a point and ignoring the evidence."
