#288055 Tiggers Are Great Thu, 27th Sep '12 7:12:54 AM

Now this here is the very first episode to star Spike, and I am glad that this show has avoided subjecting him to Positive Discrimination! That, and the Dick Dastardly (the mustache-twirling silent movie era villain) getup was a nice touch!

So I'm wondering...did anyone like Owlowiscious? Because I have to say that I didn't like him. Why? Well, let's see:

  1. His name, for starters. You have "Owlowiscious", "Owloysius", "Aloysius", "Owlicious", and some even thought they were hearing "Owl Wishes"! Spell My Name with an S is an aggravating trope at times, and the official(?) spelling of his name makes him sound pompous and pretentious.
  2. The fact that he's The Ace. To me, that sort of character has to be used carefully. Since I like Spike, I found that owl constantly being one step ahead of Spike to be spiteful and annoying.
  3. That owl rotated his head 180 degrees and I'm in agreement with Spike when he says, "Dude! That's creepy!" Can owls really do that?
  4. As you may have noticed in the clip, there was a brief shot showing that owl as a silhouette and his eyes glowing. I was so sure that owl was going to turn out to be evil. It didn't happen, making this a Fauxshadow. Since I didn't like the owl so much, I feel that They Wasted a Perfectly Good Plot.
  5. That owl never talks, and just says "Hoo", and naturally a number of characters think he's saying "Who?" A character that doesn't talk is not necessarily a bad thing, but in this case, it prevented the owl from having any personality to speak of.

Frankly, I'm glad the owl hasn't made many appearances!
