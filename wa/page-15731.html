#393266 Pannic Sat, 24th Aug '13 6:06:48 PM
I think it's just a fanfiction thing in general. It's very different from books. With books, they generally trim things down to the most important details so you know what's happening.

With a lot of fanfictions, they detail everything that happens.

For example, in the first Harry Potter book it details what generally happens in a class, and it will have scenes of classes where things happen that are significant to the plot, but apart from that it just says how the classes go and then proceeds with the passage of time, assuring the reader that nothing outrageous happens in the meantime. If it were a fanfic it'd feel the need to go into every day's routine and we'd get a detailed look at every class session, pretty much.

Concision is not a strong point with fanfiction. Or maybe it's just that fanfictions simply tend to cover shorter, denser periods of time.
