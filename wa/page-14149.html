And that's how I lost control of my life!

#353721 d Roy Thu, 4th Apr '13 10:35:13 AM from Buffalo, New York
How I became part of the fandom (notice how I am deliberately avoiding using the term brony; don't ever call me one):

I initially avoided even considering checking the show out. Not because I thought it was too idealistic girly - one my favorite series of all time is ARIA, that should be enough - but because back then all the info I had were the previous generations and the art looked just horrible.

Then I came across the legendary "Flutterguy" clip, which was (in my opinion) THE funniest moment from this series until Ponyville Confidential.

My reaction to the pilot was rather mixed, but good enough. The whole plot was, to be frank, a load of horse sense in my standards. However, during the very first couple of minutes after the intro, such as Twilight running toward the library (Canterlot library?) and floating around bunch of books, mesmerized me with its fluid animation that I've never seen before in other cartoons (admittedly I don't watch a lot of western animated series).

To be honest, though, the pilot, the first season, and really, the series as a whole is pretty much So Okay, It's Average for me. What sealed the deal was actually the fanworks, which just cannot be described.

So yeah, there is that. It's a good show. Never great, and never will be, but absolutely enjoyable.
