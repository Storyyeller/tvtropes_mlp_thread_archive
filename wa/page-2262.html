#56531 Japanese Teeth Mon, 19th Dec '11 3:39:50 PM from Meinong's jungle
Twilight is seen as the group's de facto leader because she's the one who basically brought the circle of friends together. And in that regard it makes sense; she's both relatively level headed and intelligent, so she can handle all the other ponies in a social setting. She'd work wonders a a committee chair, but she makes a pretty field leader because she can't think on her feet; she puts up a plan, and is kinda lost things deviate from it.

Here's how I'd break down the leadership of the group:

  1. Twilight - most level headed and intelligent. Capable of empathizing with the other ponies, very good at delegating responsibilities. Primary weakness is her trouble with improvising.

  2. Applejack - better at improvising, has plenty of common sense, and can keep the other ponies reasonably in line. She keeps Sweet Apple Acres going, so she must have decent time management and/or organizational skills, even if she stinks at math. Primary weakness is stubbornness; she'd probably butt heads with the other ponies if they don't like her plans.

  3. Rarity - Good time management, and is a hard worker, but her priorities get out of whack pretty easily. She could probably pull it off in a hitch if she had to, but would definitely have some issues.

  4. Every other pony - Fluttershy is too weak-willed to be a good leader; the other ponies would probably walk all over her because she wouldn't want to ever use her authority. She could probably come up with good plans, but would be unable to put them into effect. Rainbow Dash is too impulsive and probably wouldn't listen to other ponies' plans, and Pinkie...well that should be obvious.
