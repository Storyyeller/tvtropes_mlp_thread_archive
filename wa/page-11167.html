#279167 Perpetual Lurker Tue, 4th Sep '12 11:19:58 AM
Something I forgot to mention is that there is a distinct difference between character crossovers and concept crossovers. Character crossovers are those that have characters from both works interacting (i.e. these Dresden Files crossovers). Concept crossovers are those that merely fuse the settings or introduce a key mechanic of one setting to change the other (i.e. Fallout Equestria). Concept crossovers don't rely on franchise knowledge to tell a good story, most of the time, but risk running horribly askew to the tone(s) of the source materials. Character crossovers rely on the reader knowing about the characters involved, most of the time, but since the settings remain intact, the their tones are less likely to be muddled (though it can still happen).

The problem being, of course, that it's really hard to hit that balance. And I have to admit, I'm not big into crossovers for many of the same reasons Perpetual brought up. The other issue I have with a lot of it is that in a lot of cases, bringing in characters from another game is going to futz up the tone of one or both works in a way that I don't really care for. E.G. in a hypothetical Warhammer 40,000 crossover, either the lightheartedness of the ponies is going to get shot to hell really fast, or else the grimdark of 40K is going to be nonexistent. And yes, I realize that A Us can get around that issue, but I vastly prefer stories that explore how the characters would realistically (meaning consistent with what we know of their characterization) react to new situations, and overhauling it like that doesn't really interest me. Pretty much all of my favorite fics are Original Flavor.

The only thing that can get me over that obstacle is Rule of Funny.
