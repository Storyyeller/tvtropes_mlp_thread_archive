#272802 Japanese Teeth Wed, 22nd Aug '12 11:59:55 AM from Meinong's jungle
I'd also like to point out that unless you're really popular, an author doesn't really make that much more from a hard copy than an ebook. In some cases, they make less, because with a paper copy you have to pay for materials, distribution, printing, and other sorts of costs that don't exist in a digital format. It also makes self-publishing much easier and allows stuff to get out there that might not have made it through a traditional publisher. And this is coming from a guy who much prefers reading actual books to reading ebooks. (That old-book smell that libraries have is like the best thing ever)

@Blue: Ditto. I much prefer actual books to ebooks, but as long as the physical media doesn't vanish, I don't see any problem with people who think ebooks are more convenient.

That said, a physical book does have a lot less potential distractions.
