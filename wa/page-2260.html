Hold on there Fran, I think that's a bit of a class issue, isn't it? Like in D+D, a sorceror can only take a certain amount of skills because those are the spells they're sort of 'born' with (bear in mind here my knowledge of D+D is fairly minimal, largely relegated to what I absorbed when building in the Never Winter engine for school), but a Wizard can learn any spell because they get them by learning. So in essence having 'magic' for a cutie mark means that your a wizard as opposed to the unicorns base sorcerer; you can learn any spell you like, but you have to learn them. And to be fair Twilight did learn most of those spells through study, rather than through just intuitively knowing them.

Honestly, the whole 'you only get magic that relates to your talent' thing sort of bothers me. There are spell books - lots and lots of spell books, full of different sorts of spells. There are classes of spells. How common would magic as a special talent have to be for that? What I think it means is that there are a few spells you happen to know as an intuitive knack - as in, an actual talent. A unicorn could still learn a spell with study the way Twilight does, they just happen to know one or two right off the bat in a way a person who is naturally gifted at art knows one or two tricks intuitively. Twilight's 'talent' in magic just happens to be her hack-level power.
