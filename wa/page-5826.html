#145641 Bluespade Tue, 3rd Apr '12 10:26:06 PM from Texas
@Perpetual Lurker: Your rules for Attributes seem kind of wonky to me. Starting with 60 points means that if you want your character to be good at anything, they absolutely have to go below average in one of the attributes. It seems to really pretty much require the players to do a lot of stat dumping in order to make a character with decent attributes, which is weird cause I remember you saying you wanted to discourage stat dumping.

In addition, I think the maximum number for attributes should definitely be higher than 16. An attribute of 16 only confers a +3 bonus. Since we'll be rolling D20s (at least, I assume we will, since it mentions being a D20 system... you should really make that clear) a +3 bonus is not really a big change to the randomness of the roll. Since we can only get a +1, +2, or +3 bonus, we'll only be able change the outcome by 5-15%. This leaves most outcomes up to chance more than the character's ability, so even a character who is meant to be extremely strong would still have the outcome of their strength checks be mostly random.

Quoting from the rules: "having less than six points in an attribute is extremely unhealthy, and can be detrimental to the basic functioning of your character, and having more than 16 is simply unnatural without time spent training"

The math simply doesn't support this. Having a 6 in an attribute is only a -4 penalty against a d20 roll. And a +4 (18 attribute) only increases the roll by 20%, which is good but not what I would consider unnatural.
