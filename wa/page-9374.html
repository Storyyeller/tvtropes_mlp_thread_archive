#234335 kegisak Mon, 25th Jun '12 11:34:23 AM
Well, some would argue that Now and Then, Here and There didn't have a point; that it was merely an excercise in crafting emotion. Which from an artistic standpoint is valid, but not much for the reader.

On the other hand, I think it actually DID have a message. I didn't find that things were quite so black and white as I'd heard they were supposed to be. I saw rather distinct shades of gray in as lot of the characters, and I could empathize with them.

I think the point of it was to show that just because someone does a bad thing, doesn't make them a bad person. The situation you're in can shape how you act out of necessity, and even a truly good person can do abhorrent things. Sometimes a flase promise can even turn them into a bad person by constantly breaking them down.

It also shows that it doesn't have to be that way. For everything bleak that happens in it, Now and Then, Here and There is firmly idealistic. It's easy to get broken down and become a cruel person, or do evil things, but if you stand firm you can make things better.
