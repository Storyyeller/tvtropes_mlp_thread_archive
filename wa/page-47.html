Anyway, this episode was great. I was a little disappointed with Pinkie's song, but the ridiculous presentation and lampshade hanging made up for it.

Oh, and as to the person asking about the difference between Ponychan and 4chan...

4chan is... Well there's quite a lot of people who can give you their take on it. It's pretty well known. However, each board has a different culture. /b/ is easily the most hostile, but even then you'd be hard-pressed to find a sizable thread in which every poster is being an enormous tool. It also moves very fast, so it can be hard to keep a thread alive. There are pony threads on that board, but it tends to get spammed with gore. /co/ is the thread for comics and cartoons, and is the only other board that allows pony threads, but even then it only allows one at a time and prohibits furry crap or porn of the show. Last I checked, they also prohibited using pony pictures as avatars. Before the new rules, there used to be quite a bit more distasteful pictures, but since /co/ is a SFW board, they were kept under spoilers. There tends to be some arguing, even between bronies, and it tends to be mostly about fan-fics, art, and shipping.

Since Ponychan allows for more threads, it's much more easier to keep a conversation on topic, and there are less restrictions (though porn is still restricted). Because the topics are divided, threads move much more slowly than pony threads on 4chan. Also, the wordfilter, while charming, can at times hamper legitimate communication. (e.g. "gay" —> "pony"; "hate" —> "love"). Regarding the culture, users are a lot more sensitive than on 4chan and will be quick to remind you that hate won't be tolerated. Hell, I've even been temp-banned, and that's never happened to me before elsewhere. It's like you have to deal with a bunch of Rarities and Fluttershies. I guess to complete the analogy, 4chan has more Gildas, Pinkie Pies, and Rainbow Dashes?

Of course, take this with a lick of salt and a glass of water, since I haven't used these sites as much as some.
