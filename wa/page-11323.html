How is an animated TV series' release schedule remotely comparable to that of an indie videogame series?

They're not. Which can cause some rather disproportionate problems when the fandoms of both of them undertake similar-scale projects but one has a far shorter relevant time scale to do it in.

That's not even getting into the drastically different attitudes toward fanworks between the two sides of the Pacific. Western fanworks have to be nonprofit or they get descended upon by angry law griffons (and sometimes they get shut down anyway), and that as many people have gotten away with small-time commissioned stuff over the ponies is incredibly unusual given our atmosphere of aggressive litigation; compare to doujin markets that can occasionally operate on a paid professional scale.

We're at a worrying handicap in comparison is all.
