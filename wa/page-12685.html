#317111 kegisak Sat, 15th Dec '12 8:11:52 AM
Oh good, you're all finally done.

That was definitely a good episode. I'd say just a tad below the last episode, but it had a very season 1 feel to it, in terms of the plot. I'm not sure how to describe it any other way, really. Character Growth aside, I could honestly see this mixed into a pile of S1 episodes and not pick it out, whereas I would be able to pick out any given S2 episode from the S1s. Less emphasis on humour, I think, then simply telling a compelling story. Even the Pinkie subplot tying in at the end was a really good trick, and not something you see a lot in cartoons.

I really liked the fact that LD wasn't just a blanket evil or jerk character. I like that she started off looking genuinely good, and he meanness only came through development. Being ambivalent that she endangered the lives of five ponies was a bit of a jump, but beyond that everything else was a really solid progression. I also really like that they gave her a bit of a 'signature' with that stall drop of hers. That was a neat touch.

Interestingly, I actually thought the animation was pretty basic, though the episode doesn't suffer for it by any means. I just found it interesting that, in an episode where they had the opportunity for really huge animation moments, what with the flying and all, they didn't really push it all that hard. I mean, we didn't even get many new ponies - although it was pretty nice to see all our favourite pegasi from Hurricane Fluttersy, and Orion, and Omigoshomigosh that white pegasus with the curly black mane was SO CUTE! Plus it was nice to see Spitefire again, and in military duds no less!

There was definitely a lot to love in this episode, and while nothing really stuck out about it, except for the overall solidness of the writing, it's been one of the most generally, completely good episodes I've seen.
