#312818 Japanese Teeth Thu, 6th Dec '12 8:34:50 PM from Meinong's jungle
Just watched Winter Wrap Up with commentary.

Two interesting points that came up:

1. They actually had a couple reasons for using images rather than text on the checklists. We already know about the fact that it makes it easier on a young audience and that it expedites sending it overseas, but what I didn't think about was timing. The rule of thumb for onscreen text is apparently that you need to leave the text onscreen long enough that you can read it at least twice. Pictures process faster, meaning they don't need to eat up valuable screentime by holding the camera on text.

2. The "official" rule on which animals can talk is apparently "hoofed mammals and mythological creatures" can talk, and all other animals are just animals. I think we've heard that somewhere else, but this is straight from the source.
