#323873 Perpetual Lurker Sun, 30th Dec '12 11:09:49 PM
This isn't linguistic drift, though. This is just straight-up mistaken identity. People have latched on to the "punk" aspect of steampunk, ditching the far more defining "steam" aspect. What that picture actually seems to be (going by the machine guns and the rifles and the like), is Clockpunk, which is about clockwork tech, not steam tech. Yes, they're close, but they're separate for a reason. Steampunk has the iconic pipes and boilers aesthetic, while Clockpunk has the gears and pulleys aesthetic.

The airships might help, if they were actual steam powered airships and not just floating sailing ships.
