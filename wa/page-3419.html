@ L Mage I know they can be ideas or forces, that's not the problem. The problem is that your definition defines protagonist and antagonist by change, when that simply isn't the case. A protagonist can go through a story without changing (Applejack in the most recent episode is a great example), and an antagonist can just as easily be changed by the protagonist as they can change it instead. Furthermore, if the protagonist is defined by their relationship to the antagonist, then that means there can't be a protagonist without an antagonist, and that is most certainly not true either.
