Okay, the plot of Chicago: It's set in the 1920s, and there's a woman named Roxie Hart. She wants to break into showbiz, and she's carrying on an affair with a guy who claims he has connections. He reveals that he basically made the whole thing up so he could get into her pants, and she shoots him. Meanwhile, Velma Kelly, a nightclub performer that Roxie Hart admires, is arrested for murdering her husband/manager and her sister/performance partner. They both go to prison and are almost certain to get the death penalty. Except for one thing: Billy Flynn, a celebrity criminal defense lawyer who has never lost a case.

Roxie manages to procure Flynn's assistance, and his main mode of operation? Turn Roxie Hart into a media darling.
