#290334 kegisak Wed, 3rd Oct '12 3:45:03 PM
I have no comics to give, alas, but here's a joke I thought might MAKE for a good comic eventually.

Great Moments in technology: the telephone!

Invented by Alexander Gaham Bull, the telephonic communication device, or 'telephone' for short, was poised to take the world by storm! No longer would we have to rely on pegasi couriers and mail to relay information, we would be able to directly communicate with another pony. anywhere in the world! With faster communication technology would spring ahead in leaps and strides!

The device was devilishly simple - little more than a can on a string, really. A really, really long string! The user would speak into one end of the device, and that sound would travel down the line to come out of the receiving end's ear piece, and vice versa! The user would be able to talk and listen at the same time! Or at least, talk and pretend to listen.

The device had merely one enemy: The design for the dial. You see the device relied on a dial in order to choose which other telephone it would go to! At first Bull tried to use a rotary dial, which required ponies to spin a wheel to a specific set of numbers. Alas, our hooves were too big to work the dial. So he tried buttons! but our hooves were too big for those too.

With costs mounting due to failed prototypes - and a strange rash of pegasi in rubber suits repeatedly crashing into the test lines - Bull was forced to give up on his invention, and the communication revolution he had so desperately wished to start.
