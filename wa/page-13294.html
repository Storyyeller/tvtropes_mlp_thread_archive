#332342 Meta Four Fri, 25th Jan '13 10:53:26 AM from riding the wave
I may not be a writer, but my recommendation? Just jump in and write. The rest will be dealt with as it comes.
I am a (fanfic) writer and I definitely agree with this. Some amount of planning is good, but one eventually reaches a point of diminishing returns, and the planning just becomes a way to procrastinate on actually doing.

For the story I'm writing, I had an outline for what I wanted to do, and a very vague idea for my ending. Feedback from my pre-readers made me completely overhaul my planned ending‚Äîand this was feedback just on my first chapters. I hadn't told anyone how I planned to end the story, but comments about "What the heck is the significance of this?" made realize that a bunch of Cow Tools in my story could be repurposed as Chekhovs Guns.

Even aside from pre-reader feedback, some of the characters changed as I wrote them. I needed a minor antagonist for a scene in the first chapter, and I picked Cloudchaser solely because I really like her mane. Then I reused Cloudchaser in a later chapter, because I wanted to show that she wasn't a jerk 24/7, and she ended up coming across like she was just unhappy with her job. Then I used that as a punchline in a later chapter. And Carrot Top! I didn't have any personality planned for her, but feedback helped me turn her from the Straight Man into a prankster in her own right, and the sort of pony who can say ridiculous things with a completely straight face.
