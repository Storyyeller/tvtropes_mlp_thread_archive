@Seraph I thought it was entertaining but VERY flawed.

I don't think it's my villain obsession here that makes me say that the queen and her brother are easily, far and away the most interesting and sympathetic characters in the movie. It feels like the filmakers were far more interested in them than in any of the good guys. and they were both IMPOSSIBLY badass.

The plot is VERY predictable (though one villainous moment where the queen disguises herself as the prince to trick Snow White into eating the apple almost surprised me) and all attempts at romance are pathetic (though, honestly I thought the lack of romance was intentional.) and the script has this weird habit of setting up something PURELY so it can set up the next part of the plot (for example, the dark forest that Snow White runs into is only in the film for about... 15 minutes, and only exists to give the queen a reason to need the huntsman. After Snow and the huntsman meet, they leave the forest incredibly quickly. It's a plot point that existed PURELY to justify a certain character's involvement in the plot.)

And Kristen Stewart really wasn't THAT bad in the movie. Better than she was in Twilight, but there's a point in the movie where she gives this big rallying speech to her troops and you JUST don't buy it... at all. She doesn't have anywhere near the level of gravitase needed to pull off a speech like that

But it was good. REALLY, I enjoyed it. It had a badass (seriously, the heroes don't dominate ANY fights in this entire movie), sympathetic villain. Purely for that, I didn't regret watching it.
