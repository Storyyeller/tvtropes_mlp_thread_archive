@ginger: I think the treatment of the hero and the villain in a story are generally one of the biggest parts of making a compelling story. Sympathetic P.O.V. I think is neccessary for both, albeit with careful balance. If the hero is only sympathetic, people will accuse the villain of being generic and a cardboard For the Evulz type that taints the motive and build up of the whole battle. If the villain is more sympathetic, it makes it harder to enjoy the ending or even worse risks making the hero look like an unrootable 'bully'. Like you said, a well rounded universe is important to make all aspects of a story enjoyable. So many medias have suffered due to overfocusing on one aspect too much.
