I have to say I do regard the whole Like Reality Unless Noted thing...except that in 90% of the cases the "like noted" clause basically blows established physics out of the water. My overall take is that the physical laws of nature as we know them do exist, but that magic is able to circumvent them so easily they they're ultimately more guidelines than anything else. Like there is a law of conservation of mass and energy in Equestria, it's just that magic can overcome it, so it's not an immutable law in the same way it is in our universe.

So Like Reality Unless Noted basically means anything that deviates from real world science in the setting can be attributed to magic.

@Kegisak: If I ever did an ask blog (which I won't because I don't have the time and can't draw) I'd try to subvert the whole sadness element of it. Like I'd pick the most unnecessarily tear-jerky theme I could think of, but then focus on the pony in question overcoming the issue fairly quickly and learning to deal with it. Like I'd have a tumblr where Twilight's horn gets broken off, but rather than have her angst endlessly about it, it would focus on her attempts to adjust to life without magic. There's be some sadness, sure, but it would more about stuff like her having difficulty getting books off of her shelves without levitation or whatnot.
