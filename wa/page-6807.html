Oh! Something interesting I noticed when watching the G Gundam parody again.

When Cadance and Shining Armor perform the big love-powerd shield wave thing, take a look at Cadance's mane during the whole sequence. For that short time, it flows, much like Celestia's mane. At one point, even her tail flows. However, it settles back into a solid shape when the spell is over and they settle on the ground.

So, the flowing mane is definitely an indication of power. However, Cadance is younger than Celestia or Luna, and hasn't reached full maturity yet. It's only in moments like that that her mane flows.

I think the best analogy I can think of is to think to Dragon Ball Z. It's like the golden hair of the Super Saiyan state. Meanwhile, Celestia and Luna are like Goku in the Cell Saga: they're powerful enough that they're in this powerful state at all hours of the day.

#170162 Enlong Mon, 23rd Apr '12 11:08:41 AM from The Magitek Holyland
In a society with magic, magical development is often seen supplanting technological advancement that we had in real life. Fullmetal Alchemist would be a good example. With alchemy forming the basis of the socity, the country of Amestris saw many advancements through use of alchemical methods, rather than pure technological advancement. The same can most likely apply to Equestrian society. As such, a steam engine would likely be less of a major revolution, and more of a slightly different way of doing things.

Back on the subject of flowing manes, which I get the feeling that nobody else is particularly interested in, I like the way they've shown us how it works without telling us directly. We saw the marked change in Luna's look, which suggested she was weakened in her first appearance, or simply grew older and more powerful in the previous year. Her flowing mane resembles Nightmare Moon's who was clearly more powerful than Luna. We saw Celestia's mane becoming static when she was knocked out by Chrysalis, suggesting that it can be seen as a sort of indicator of power, and we saw Cadance's mane briefly take on insubstantial properties during the big scene, solidifying the whole thing. It just works very nicely, and they didn't have to say a word.
