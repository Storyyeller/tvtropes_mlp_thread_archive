#152787 Psi 001 Tue, 10th Apr '12 7:09:58 PM
I think Depending on the Writer is kinda neccesary to some extent for real Character Development. A lot of times one person will have restricted outlooks of how a character acts and feels (or in some cases just not be interested in them at all), so having a batch of different people giving ideas and perspective to it actually helps create multi faceted characters and scenarios. Truth be told a lot times people act erratically in real life anyway. I know I do.

Often times whenever a franchise becomes limited to one creative mind Flanderization or limitation in concepts often kicks in. eg. most of the Looney Tunes characters and formulas when Chuck Jones' personas became the definitive renditions rather than a contributing one.
