I don't see how this is a throwaway just because there was no lesson learned. I mean there was still a very obvious moral, it's just that AJ knew it already.

The only thing that bothers me about this episode is that, with a bit of reasonable haggling, this could have been solved with a significant benefit to both sides. Flim and Flam need the apples to make cider, right? And they can make it far faster than AJ and her family could. Now, the apple family needs the profit from Cider to keep their business afloat through the winter, but here's the thing: they have to use apples to make that cider. Which means that the issue is likely that demand for apples themselves goes down during the winter, not that they run out of apples, otherwise they could just make the same profits by selling the apples.

But now, who could they maybe sell those apples to?

It's two bits a pop for a mug of cider. That comes out to at least a couple apples, which means we're looking at a rough price of say, a bit to an apple. Sell all those leftover apples to Flim and Flam. They mark the price up a bit (two, three bits to a mug), and sell that. Or, mark off a section of the orchard for Flim and Flam to take from for a reasonable price. Enough to keep you afloat through the winter. They'd be making the same amount of money, if not more, and Flim and Flam could set up a nice business and spend their spare time industrializing ponyville with their godawesome steampunk(magepunk?) technology.

The apple Family: Good at apples. Bad at business.
