No, beauty and reason are most certainly not incompatible. Just because two scientists happen to be cynics doesn't mean that every scientist, or even any person who applies reason is a cold, unfeeling machine who only cares about physical elements. It does not hurt the beauty of a sunset to know a little bit about it. I know why the sky turns orange and pink and purple at twilight. I know that it's because of the sun's light being refracted through tiny particles of ice and dust suspended in the air. Does that mean that all I see is tiny particles in the air? No, it doesn't. I see a beautiful painting. I see the perfect end to a day. I know why it happens, but that doesn't make it not beautiful. And just because I understand that clouds are nothing more than water vapour, doesn't mean I can't see shapes in them, or that I can't find the subtle shades in them wonderful.

And for what it's worth, I'm not even talking about anti-religion here. You can be religious and still apply reason. I happen to know a biblical scholar - someone who analyzes the bible using reason, to determine what in it is and isn't supposed to be taken as truth. He went to university, and studied for it. Do you know what his profession is. He's a pastor. A man who regularly applies reason to religion, still believes in it enough to preach it.

It bears repeating: Knowing why something is amazing, doesn't make it not amazing. When we know nothing about something, it's mysterious and fantastic. When we know a little about something, it becomes dull. But when we know a lot about something it becomes fantastic again, just because of how amazing, how unlikely and how random the chance of it happening really, truly is. It becomes even more amazing for all the tiny little details that go into it. Maybe some people measure the merigolds, but that doesn't mean that everybody does. Because I'll tell you what, I sure as hell don't. I want to know everything about everything. I want to know what makes the sun set, and what makes the flowers grow and the birds sing. And do you know why I want to learn about all those things? Because they're beautiful.
