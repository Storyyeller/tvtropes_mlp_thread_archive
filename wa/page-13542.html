On a somewhat unrelated note, I'm personally getting tired of some fans confusing girlieness and shallowness as if they're the same thing. Yes, FIM is one of the most girly, cute, and idealistic shows ever made. It's an esthetic as well as financially viable choice on Hasbro's part, good writing and characterization have nothing to do with it.

Yeah, I'm not worried about girliness. FFS, I like Rarity.

My concern is the shallowness. This show worked because the staff went to an immense amount of trouble building up a breathing world, and unusually complex characters whose most important traits stem directly from that worldbuilding and from twisting otherwise familiar archetypes in creative ways to fit into that alien setting in a way that no other setting could.

Turning it into a cheap fanfiction style High School AU eviscerates all of that. You lose the interesting and alien setting. You make them too young to have the adult jobs that in many ways define them and the world around them. You remove pretty much everything that made them meaningful characters and reduce them to a voice and a hairstyle.
