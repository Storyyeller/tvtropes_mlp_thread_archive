@Fallen: It depends on context. As a party member, unless they're young they'd be over powered. As an enemy, they could be extremely threatening, and a good enemy. Enemies in RPG's often tend to be world-shattering, because progression and ginding means that by games end you're felling giants like they're nothing.

A pacifist main character has never been the issue, it's always the gameplay issue. Like, stealth games encourage you not to fight by having pretty shitty combat in general. But most games are very action-oriented. I think as technology advances we'll find ways around that (Like a game about directing others to do your bidding, and not actually doing any killing yourself), but it's very difficult because, unfortunately, a lot of gamers just go for power fantasy stuff.

Here's a thought: A main character is a Guile Hero game. All combat takes place in sripted locations, and as opposed to fighting directly you need to set off traps and solve quick puzzles to defeat the enemies.
