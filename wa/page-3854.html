Sorry, I still disagree. Creativity does not equal good writing (unfortunately). J.R. Tolkien and H.P. Lovecraft were both immensely creative people who were geniuses when it came to world building and original flavor. Both of them created immensely influential and intriguing worlds that continue to inspire people to this day. Both of them created brand new genres. And both of them (in my opinion) were absolutely horrendous writers. Neither of them could craft a compelling character or write entertaining dialogue to save their lives.

Though I have no examples (again, I don't read fanfic), I think it is perfectly possible and quite likely that there exists an author out there who is a brilliant and genius writer, but, for whatever reason, is completely incapable of designing his/her own unique world. This hypothetical person wouldn't be any less of a writer for writing in an established setting... they'd just have a lot more trouble making a living off it.
