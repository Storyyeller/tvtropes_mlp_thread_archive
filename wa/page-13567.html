AJ was fine that episode. The problem was that none of the family reacted to her. The main conflict of the episode was supposed to be "AJ ruins the family reunion by overplanning", but said family was so willing to go with it that it came across as "AJ mildly annoys her family by overplanning".

  • Applebuck Season is the best, as it actually focuses on AJ for a good chunk of time.

  • SSCS 6 K was a problem in that AJ was mostly sidelined in favor for the rest of her family.

If they would build an episode that focused entirely around AJ having an issue with somepony, and followed her for the whole time, it could work easily.

#339174 Enlong Thu, 14th Feb '13 10:15:14 PM from The Underground Facility
Sombra has the potential to be a very interesting villain. We got a lot of character in retrospect from that two-parter.

He's exceedingly arrogant, he chose to rule through fear and set things up around it (the only entrance to the Heart Chamber was in his throne room. Nobody would dare come before him)
He was very prepared. The staircase in the throne room could only be opened with shadow magic, but using that same magic on the Scary Door would set it off. Anyone without magic or wings (that is, everyone in the Crystal Empire) would have been exhausted by the climb to the heart, and nobody but a flier could get to the heart without triggering the floor trap.
And he's freaky resiliant and determined (horn snaps off; he uses it to corrupt the inside of the shield and just grows another. Though that could be living shadow stuff)

It's a shame that none of it could be expressed from an amorphous shadow, and most of it was inferred from what he left behind. He would be a great villain in his prime, which is why many of us were hoping that he would return in some capacity by the end of the season.

Though, personally, I still think it's possible, though I get the feeling that whatever is being built up to with Discord's reformation is bigger than him.

#339175 Perpetual Lurker Thu, 14th Feb '13 10:15:36 PM
@Kegi Actually, I think that the problem is that she's too developed, so much so that she's become hard to write for. Right from the get-go, she's the most mature and grounded of the group, and the vast majority of her development was explored in Season 1 with Applebuck Season, Look Before You Sleep, and Fall Weather Friends. Almost everything past that point becomes a reaffirmation of her past development, or a use of her involvement to spotlight her family.

Basically, she needs an arc like Twilight and Rainbow Dash, since she's been almost fully explored and fleshed out by this point. Problem is that she has no long term goals. She's extremely content with her life, since the biggest ambition she's ever shown is to make money to maintain her life, not to experience anything new or advance in any way. Something big needs to shake her up, otherwise she has nowhere else to go.
