#8678 Erik S Sat, 2nd Jul '11 1:24:19 AM
I don't do shipping. I tend to view it as the distaff counterpart to "could Hulk beat Superman", so when shipping discussions get serious I am a little dumbfounded. But I am never a very passionate fan and find any kind of heated discussion around a cartoon, no matter how cleverly made, a bit odd and off-putting.

I have one little gripe with same-sex shipping, and it has nothing against same-sex relationships IRL (I have been in them and is in one right now) but that there is an old cliché, when you have an unattached man and an unattached woman in a story, they HAVE to get in a relationship. That's annoying because it implies the only relationship a man and a woman can have is a romantic one. With same-sex relationships more acceptable, it sometimes seems the ONLY relationship ANYONE can have is a romantic one. Because if you express any kind of closeness to anyone it means you want to hump their bones. The shipping fans of this show reminds me of Azumanga Daioh (both shows having all interesting characters being female), where people would say that OF COURSE they would have to be lesbians, since no one has a straight relationship. Well, I was part of a circle of maybe eight friends in high school: one guy had a girlfriend at all during three years, one guy showed interest in the same sex (thats me). The other guys were just dateless and fairly OK with it. This is in no way uncommon in real life, and why shouldnt it be in fiction? Especially since both shows - Friendship is Magic and Azumanga Daioh - are great stories about NON-romantic relationships to begin with. (And I consider the main cast adults and have been known to look at furry smut, so I dont have a problem with their age or species per se, even if the shipping should get explicit. It is the idea that it is NEEDED.)

But I shouldn't gripe. I can avoid shipping fics - I even read them willingly. It's just that the thoughts of some shippers sit wrong with me.
