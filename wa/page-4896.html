#122381 Japanese Teeth Mon, 12th Mar '12 9:55:38 AM from Meinong's jungle
Once again C. S. Lewis comes through. His definition of "magic" was "objective efficacy that cannot be further analyzed". In that you can repeatedly observe it and predict it because it's usage is always the same, which is why Twilight is capable of studying spells and whatnot. The difference is that you can't necessarily break down the nitty-gritty of why it occurs that way. Like you can say "performing spell X always has result Y" because it's an observable phenomena, but you can't necessarily not they exact connection. Like if we knew that you get a spark when you strike flint and steel together but didn't know the chemical reactions or physics that caused it.
