#352209 Pykrete Sat, 30th Mar '13 2:02:34 PM from Viridian Forest
The most effective way to create a conflict between two characters is to pit their strongest qualities and flaws against each other — and bonus points when they match! The strongest qualities of Twi and AJ is that they're both generally sane and reasonable, albeit from opposite standpoints (intellectual vs. straightforward). Their greatest flaws are that Twilight is sometimes obliviously tactless and Applejack can be goaded into a temper — both of which can lead into an accidental vindictive streak.

So put them both up against an external problem, but give them each only half the solution...then watch them obstruct each other. Twilight's perfectionism causes her to become hostile, which touches off Applejack's pride and sets her into stubborn bullheaded mode. Twilight goes all Magical Lone Wolf to do it herself and fails, while Applejack pulls the town together into a group effort that degrades into a misaimed riot.

I like the idea of an Applejack vs. Fluttershy episode where Applejack's methods of corralling critters and her being used to more organized farming come in conflict with Fluttershy's more laidback approach to the animals she tends to. This was hinted at in Applebuck Season where Applejack accidentally made the rabbits panic because she aggressively herded them like cattle but it could be used again. This was also hinted at in Keep Calm and Flutter On where Applejack needed Fluttershy's help with dealing with the beavers, who were annoyed partially because Applejack just insulted them and didn't try to look at things from the beavers' point of view. Basically, Applejack is used to making animals do things for the sake of the farm, while Fluttershy is used to doing things that the animals need even if it causes problems for her.

And of course how Rarity deals with Twilight being a princess now, given Rarity loves anything related to royalty.

I would also like to see some of the Equestrian holidays be celebrated again.
